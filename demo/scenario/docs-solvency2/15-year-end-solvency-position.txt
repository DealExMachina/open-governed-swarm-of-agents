1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Meridian Insurance Group AG reports a year-end SCR ratio of 151%,
representing a significant recovery from the low point of 128% at
Q1 2026 but below the revised Board target of 155%.

The Solvency and Financial Condition Report (SFCR) for FY2026 has
been prepared for filing with BaFin by 7 April 2027 and simultaneous
public disclosure per Art. 51 of Directive 2009/138/EC.

This summary consolidates the full-year solvency journey and provides
the definitive figures for the FY2026 regulatory reporting cycle.

8. OUTSTANDING MATTERS AND FORWARD LOOK

8.1 Contract Boundary Classification (Audit Observation 4)
  Parallel calculations under both interpretations have been prepared:
    Current interpretation (5-year boundary):  SCR ratio 151%
    Alternative interpretation (1-year boundary): SCR ratio 149%
  BaFin dialogue initiated on 15 November 2026. Response pending.
  The SFCR discloses this as a significant judgment in Note 4.3.

8.2 Swiss Re Rating
  Moody's concluded its review on 10 December 2026: downgraded Swiss Re
  to A2 (from Aa3), stable outlook. S&P rating remains A- (stable).
  No further SCR impact (A2/A- both map to credit quality step 3).
  Treaty renewal for 1 January 2027: novation of remaining Swiss Re
  exposure to a panel of A+-rated reinsurers approved by the Board.

8.3 EIOPA Stress Test Remediation
  BaFin acknowledged Meridian's capital remediation plan on 15 October
  2026 and noted the improved trajectory. Enhanced supervisory reporting
  (quarterly) will continue through H1 2027.

8.4 2027 Outlook
  Projected SCR ratio range: 155%-162% (assuming no further regulatory
  changes, successful Swiss Re novation, and stable markets).
  Key risk: EIOPA review of standard formula calibration scheduled
  for Q2 2027 could affect market risk and life underwriting charges.

10. CONTRADICTION AND DRIFT REGISTER

For the benefit of governance monitoring systems, the following
regulatory contradictions and drifts occurred during FY2026:

  C1: SCR ratio — 142% (Q4 2025) vs 128% (Q1 2026) vs 151% (Q4 2026)
      Each figure valid under its applicable regulatory framework.
      No restatement of prior figures.

  C2: Transitional measure — EUR 45M vs EUR 28M vs EUR 29M
      Bitemporal records maintained. Q4 2025 figure preserved.

  C3: Internal model vs standard formula — initially aligned (PIM
      3.2% lower), then diverged (PIM 1.5% higher). Reversal driven
      by look-through data revealing concentrated exposures.

  C4: Look-through approach — initially 78% coverage, then 100%.
      EIOPA guideline change (EIOPA-BoS-26/142) was the trigger.
      Retroactive assessment performed but prior figures not restated.

  C5: Reinsurance counterparty quality — Swiss Re A+ -> A-/A2.
      ORSA assumptions invalidated. SCR impact EUR 12M.

  C6: Contract boundary — two interpretations coexist. Resolution
      pending BaFin guidance. Parallel calculations disclosed.
