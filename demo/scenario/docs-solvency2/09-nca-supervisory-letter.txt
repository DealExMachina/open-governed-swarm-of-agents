1. CONTEXT

BaFin has reviewed Meridian Insurance Group AG's ("Meridian") ad hoc
report dated 30 April 2026 concerning the recalculation of the
Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR) following publication of EIOPA's
updated look-through guidelines (EIOPA-BoS-26/142).

We note that Meridian's SCR ratio has decreased from 142% (Q4 2025)
to 128% (Q1 2026), and that the Art. 308c transitional deduction has
been reduced from EUR 45,000,000 to EUR 28,000,000.

2. SUPERVISORY CONCERNS

BaFin has the following specific concerns:

2.1 Adequacy of Technical Provisions
  The reduction in the transitional measure, combined with the
  enhanced look-through revealing previously unidentified concentrated
  exposures, raises questions about the adequacy of Meridian's
  technical provisions. BaFin requests that the Appointed Actuary,
  Herr Prof. Stefan Bruckner, provide a supplementary opinion on
  the adequacy of technical provisions under Art. 48 of Directive
  2009/138/EC, specifically addressing:
  (a) Whether the look-through changes affect the best estimate
      liability calculation;
  (b) Whether the risk margin requires adjustment given the revised
      SCR;
  (c) Whether contract boundary assumptions remain appropriate.

2.2 Internal Model Alignment
  BaFin notes from the Internal Model Calibration Report (dated
  24 January 2026) that Meridian's partial internal model produced
  a LOWER SCR than the standard formula (by 3.2%). Given the
  significant changes to market risk charges under the look-through
  revision, BaFin requests confirmation that:
  (a) The internal model validation remains valid;
  (b) The internal model has been or will be recalibrated to reflect
      the enhanced look-through data;
  (c) Any divergence between internal model and standard formula
      results is explained and justified.

2.3 Governance and Risk Management
  The Internal Audit observation (IA-2026-004, Observation 1) that
  look-through data was available but not incorporated raises concerns
  about the effectiveness of Meridian's system of governance under
  Art. 41-50 of Directive 2009/138/EC. BaFin expects Meridian to
  demonstrate that:
  (a) The root cause of the data gap has been identified;
  (b) Appropriate accountability measures have been taken;
  (c) Controls have been strengthened to prevent recurrence.

3. REQUIRED ACTIONS

BaFin requires Meridian to submit the following within 90 CALENDAR
DAYS of this letter (i.e., by 20 August 2026):

3.1 Capital Remediation Plan
  A Board-approved plan to restore the SCR ratio to at least the
  internal target of 135% within a reasonable timeframe (not to
  exceed 12 months from the date of this letter).

3.2 Supplementary Appointed Actuary Opinion
  As described in Section 2.1 above.

3.3 Internal Model Update
  A plan and timeline for recalibrating the partial internal model
  to incorporate enhanced look-through data, including a gap analysis
  comparing internal model and standard formula results.

3.4 Governance Enhancement Plan
  Remedial actions addressing the governance concerns in Section 2.3,
  including updated policies, procedures, and control documentation.

4. TRANSITIONAL MEASURE CONFIRMATION

Regarding Meridian's request for confirmation of the Q4 2025
transitional figure: BaFin confirms that the Q4 2025 Art. 308c
transitional deduction of EUR 45,000,000 was calculated in accordance
with the regulatory requirements then in force and remains the
official figure for the Q4 2025 reporting period. No restatement
is required.

The revised figure of EUR 28,000,000 is acknowledged as applying
from Q1 2026 onwards, subject to formal approval by BaFin following
submission of the recalculated transitional documentation.

5. SUPERVISORY ESCALATION

BaFin notes that while the SCR ratio of 128% remains above the 100%
regulatory minimum, it is below Meridian's own internal target of
135%. Failure to submit a satisfactory remediation plan within the
stipulated timeframe may result in supervisory measures under
Art. 138-139 of Directive 2009/138/EC, including but not limited to:
  - Restriction on dividend distributions
  - Requirement to hold additional capital add-on under Art. 37
  - Enhanced supervisory reporting requirements

We expect Meridian's full cooperation and transparency throughout
this process.
