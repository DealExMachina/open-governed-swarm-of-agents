1. PURPOSE

This memorandum reviews the impact of EIOPA's updated look-through
guidelines on Meridian's Art. 308c transitional measure and determines
whether an early recalculation is required.

CRITICAL BITEMPORAL COMPLIANCE NOTE:

The Q4 2025 transitional deduction of EUR 45,000,000 (calculated as at
31 December 2025 and approved by the Supervisory Board on 20 December
2025) was CORRECTLY calculated under the regulatory framework then in
force. Specifically:

  Transaction time:  20 December 2025 (date of approval and recording)
  Valid time:        1 January 2025 to 31 December 2025 (applicable period)
  Regulatory basis:  EIOPA-BoS-14/171, BaFin Circular 2/2016 (VA)

This figure SHALL NOT be overwritten or restated. It represents the
legitimate regulatory position for the Q4 2025 period. Any subsequent
recalculation creates a NEW record with its own transaction time and
valid time, coexisting with the original.

2. TRIGGER ANALYSIS

Guideline 4.3 of EIOPA-BoS-26/142 defines materiality thresholds for
early recalculation of the transitional measure:
  - Change in SCR > 10%: Actual change = 8.0% (EUR 50M / EUR 627M)
    THRESHOLD NOT MET
  - Change in SCR ratio > 10pp: Actual change = 14pp (142% -> 128%)
    THRESHOLD MET

Since one of the two thresholds is met, BaFin has DISCRETION (but is
not obligated) to require early recalculation under Art. 308c(5).

Meridian has elected to proactively perform the recalculation to

3.3 Revised Transitional Deduction
  Difference:                            EUR    72,000,000

  Revised transitional deduction:        EUR    28,000,000

  Previous deduction (Q4 2025):          EUR    45,000,000
  Reduction:                             EUR    17,000,000

4. IMPACT ON SCR RATIO

The reduction in transitional deduction of EUR 17,000,000 increases
effective technical provisions, which flows through to the SCR via
increased risk charges on the best estimate liabilities.

  Impact on SCR: approximately EUR +5,000,000
  Impact on own funds: approximately EUR -12,000,000 (higher TP
    reduces excess of assets over liabilities)

  Adjusted SCR (including transitional impact):  EUR 682,000,000
  Adjusted own funds:                            EUR 884,000,000
  Adjusted SCR ratio:                            129.6%

  Note: The small improvement from 128% to ~130% after accounting for
  transitional changes reflects the interaction effects. The net
  position remains materially below the 135% target.

6. LEGAL ANALYSIS

Dr. Richter confirms that this approach is consistent with:
  - Art. 308c(4): Prior calculations remain valid until superseded
  - EIOPA Guideline 3.3: Retroactive assessment is informational only
  - BaFin administrative practice: No precedent for retrospective
    invalidation of previously approved transitional figures

However, there is a residual risk that BaFin may adopt a more
conservative stance given the magnitude of the change. The probability
of retrospective invalidation is assessed as LOW (< 10%).
