EUROPEAN INSURANCE AND OCCUPATIONAL PENSIONS AUTHORITY (EIOPA)
Updated Guidelines on the Application of the Look-Through Approach
Reference: EIOPA-BoS-26/142
Date of Publication: 15 April 2026
Effective Date: Immediate (15 April 2026)
Applicable to: All insurance and reinsurance undertakings subject to
  Directive 2009/138/EC

INTRODUCTORY NOTE FROM THE CHAIRPERSON

Following a comprehensive review of supervisory practices across
Member States and the findings of EIOPA's 2025 thematic review on
investment risk (Ref. EIOPA-BoS-25/389), EIOPA issues these updated
guidelines to strengthen the application of the look-through approach
for collective investment undertakings under Art. 84 of Delegated
Regulation (EU) 2015/35.

These guidelines replace EIOPA Guidelines EIOPA-BoS-14/171 (Section 7)
and apply with IMMEDIATE EFFECT.

GUIDELINE 1 — SCOPE OF APPLICATION (replaces former Guideline 12)

1.1 Undertakings shall apply the full look-through approach to ALL
    collective investment undertakings, funds of funds, and similar
    investment structures, regardless of the proportion of the
    undertaking's total assets they represent.

1.2 The mandate-based approach is NO LONGER acceptable as a primary
    methodology. It may be used only as a TEMPORARY measure (maximum
    3 months) while look-through data is being obtained, and only if
    the undertaking can demonstrate active efforts to obtain such data.

1.3 Where look-through data is genuinely unavailable after reasonable
    efforts, the undertaking shall apply the HIGHEST risk charge
    applicable to any asset class within the fund's investment mandate.

GUIDELINE 2 — DATA FREQUENCY AND TIMELINESS

2.1 Look-through data shall be refreshed at least QUARTERLY, with a
    maximum data lag of 30 calendar days from the reporting date.

2.2 Previous guidance permitted semi-annual refresh. This is hereby
    superseded. Quarterly refresh is the minimum standard.

GUIDELINE 3 — RETROACTIVE REVIEW RECOMMENDATION

3.1 EIOPA RECOMMENDS (comply or explain) that undertakings perform a
    retroactive assessment of their most recent SCR calculation to
    quantify the impact of applying these updated guidelines.

3.2 This retroactive assessment shall be communicated to the National
    Competent Authority (NCA) within 60 calendar days of the effective
    date of these guidelines (i.e., by 14 June 2026).

3.3 IMPORTANT: The retroactive assessment is for INFORMATIONAL PURPOSES
    and does NOT invalidate prior SCR calculations that were compliant
    under the then-applicable guidelines. The prior SCR figures remain
    the official regulatory figures for the relevant reporting period.

3.4 However, NCAs may, at their discretion, require undertakings to
    submit a supplementary QRT reflecting the restated position.

GUIDELINE 4 — IMPACT ON TRANSITIONAL MEASURES

4.1 The transitional measure on technical provisions under Art. 308c
    of Directive 2009/138/EC is NOT directly affected by these
    guidelines, as the look-through approach pertains to asset-side
    risk charges rather than technical provisions.

4.2 HOWEVER, where the application of enhanced look-through results in
    a material change to the undertaking's risk profile, NCAs may
    require an early recalculation of the transitional measure under
    Art. 308c(5).

4.3 The threshold for "material change" is defined as a change in the
    SCR of more than 10% or a change in the SCR ratio of more than
    10 percentage points.

GUIDELINE 5 — SUPERVISORY REPORTING

5.1 Undertakings shall include a dedicated section in their next
    Regular Supervisory Report (RSR) describing the implementation of
    these guidelines and any quantitative impact.

5.2 The Solvency and Financial Condition Report (SFCR) for the current
    reporting year shall disclose the impact of the look-through
    changes on the SCR.

COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

National Competent Authorities are required to notify EIOPA within
2 months of whether they comply or intend to comply with these
guidelines, in accordance with Art. 16(3) of Regulation (EU)
No 1094/2010.

Undertakings should direct questions to their NCA in the first
instance. EIOPA will publish an updated Q&A document within 30 days.
