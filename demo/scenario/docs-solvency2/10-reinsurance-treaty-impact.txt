1. EVENT SUMMARY

On 5 June 2026, Standard & Poor's downgraded Swiss Reinsurance Company
Ltd ("Swiss Re") from A+ (stable outlook) to A- (negative outlook),
citing increased exposure to climate-related catastrophe losses and
deterioration in reserve adequacy. This represents a two-notch
downgrade.

AM Best concurrently revised Swiss Re's Financial Strength Rating from
A+ (Superior) to A (Excellent) with a negative outlook on 6 June 2026.

Moody's has placed Swiss Re's Aa3 rating on review for downgrade.

3. SCR COUNTERPARTY DEFAULT RISK MODULE IMPACT

counterparty default risk module calculates the loss-given-default
(LGD) and probability of default (PD) for each reinsurance exposure.

7. CONTRADICTION FLAG

Note for the record: The ORSA stress scenarios (ORSA-2026-001,
Section 4) assumed all reinsurance counterparties rated A+ or above.
The Swiss Re downgrade means this assumption is NO LONGER VALID.
The ORSA projections should be updated to reflect the revised
counterparty credit quality. This represents a contradiction between
the current ORSA and actual market conditions.
