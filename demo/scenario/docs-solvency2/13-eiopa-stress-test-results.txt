1. BACKGROUND

EIOPA conducted its biennial insurance stress test in H1 2026, with
results communicated to participating undertakings on 25 August 2026.
Aggregate results will be published by EIOPA on 15 October 2026.

Meridian was included in the sample of 43 European insurance groups
(representing 75% of European insurance assets). Participation is
mandatory for groups with total assets exceeding EUR 10 billion.

Reference date: 31 March 2026 (coincides with Meridian's Q1 2026
reporting date).

2. SCENARIO DESCRIPTIONS

EIOPA prescribed three scenarios:

  BASE SCENARIO:
    Economic conditions broadly unchanged from Q1 2026.
    Interest rates: current forward curve.
    Equity markets: no shock.
    Credit spreads: no shock.
    Insurance-specific: no additional stresses.

  ADVERSE SCENARIO (Prolonged Stagflation):
    GDP: -2.5% (Eurozone), -3.8% (UK)
    Inflation: +4.2% above baseline for 24 months
    Interest rates: +350bps (short end), +150bps (long end) — inversion
    Equity markets: -45% (Type 1), -55% (Type 2)
    Credit spreads: +180bps (IG), +450bps (HY)
    Sovereign spreads: +120bps (peripheral), +30bps (core)
    Lapse shock: +15% mass lapse in unit-linked
    Expense inflation: +3.5% above baseline
    Natural catastrophe: 1-in-30 year European windstorm

  CLIMATE SCENARIO (Transition Risk):
    Carbon price: EUR 250/tonne by 2030 (accelerated transition)
    Fossil fuel equity write-down: -60%
    Real estate revaluation: -15% (energy-inefficient buildings)
    Green bond spread tightening: -40bps
    Stranded asset provisions: 5% of relevant exposures

Meridian is positioned:

This is a cause for concern. Meridian's relative positioning has
deteriorated compared to the 2024 stress test (where it was at the
45th percentile for the adverse scenario).

7. IMPLICATIONS AND REQUIRED ACTIONS

7.1 Supervisory Dialogue
  EIOPA guidelines state that undertakings failing the adverse scenario
  must engage in enhanced supervisory dialogue with their NCA. BaFin
  will likely request:
  - A detailed action plan addressing adverse scenario vulnerabilities
  - Enhanced reporting on market risk exposures (quarterly)
  - Board-level attestation that management actions are actionable

7.2 Capital Remediation Plan Update
  The existing CRP (CRP-2026-001) targets 155% by Q4 2026. The stress
  test results suggest this target may be insufficient given the
  adverse scenario impact of -34.8pp. The Board should consider
  whether a higher target is appropriate.

7.3 Public Disclosure
  When EIOPA publishes aggregate results on 15 October 2026, Meridian
  will be identifiable due to sample size. External Communications
  Monitor Herr Baumann is preparing a press statement and investor
  Q&A document.
