1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Internal Audit Function has completed its planned audit activities
for Q4 2025, covering three areas: (i) Solvency II reporting processes,
(ii) investment compliance, and (iii) reinsurance arrangements. This
report presents three observations, of which one is rated Material.

Overall assessment: PARTIALLY SATISFACTORY — the Material observation
on the look-through approach requires prompt management attention.

2. AUDIT SCOPE AND METHODOLOGY

Audit period: 1 October 2025 to 31 December 2025
Standards applied: IIA International Standards for the Professional
Practice of Internal Auditing (2024 revision)
Population tested: 47 investment funds (EUR 340,000,000 total),
12 reinsurance treaties, 8 quarterly reporting deliverables.

3. OBSERVATIONS

OBSERVATION 1 — MATERIAL
Title: Look-Through Approach for Collective Investment Undertakings
  Incomplete
Rating: MATERIAL
Risk Area: Solvency II Pillar 1 — SCR Calculation
Reference: Art. 84, Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/35

Description:
  Meridian applies the look-through approach to 78% of its fund
  holdings by value (EUR 265,200,000 of EUR 340,000,000). The
  remaining 22% (EUR 74,800,000) is treated under the mandate-based
  approach. Internal Audit identified that for 7 of the 47 funds
  (15%), the mandate-based approach was applied not due to genuine
  data unavailability but because the investment operations team had
  not requested updated fund composition data from asset managers.

  For 3 of these 7 funds (EUR 31,400,000 combined), updated look-
  through data IS available from the fund manager but had not been
  incorporated into the SCR calculation as at 31 December 2025.

Impact Assessment:
  If full look-through were applied to these 3 funds, the market risk
  sub-module could change by an estimated EUR 5,000,000 to EUR 12,000,000.
  This would not breach the SCR ratio target of 135% but represents a
  potential misstatement in the reported figures.

Management Response (Herr Thomas Lange, Head of Investment Operations):
  Accepted. Remediation plan to request quarterly look-through data
  from all fund managers by 31 March 2026. Estimated completion: Q2 2026.

OBSERVATION 2 — SIGNIFICANT
Title: Reinsurance Treaty Documentation Gaps
Rating: SIGNIFICANT
Risk Area: Solvency II Pillar 1 — Counterparty Default Risk Module

Description:
  For 2 of 12 reinsurance treaties reviewed, the signed treaty
  documentation did not match the parameters entered in the actuarial
  system (Prophet). Specifically:
  - Treaty RT-2024-007 (Swiss Re): attachment point recorded as
    EUR 50,000,000 but treaty states EUR 55,000,000.
  - Treaty RT-2024-011 (Munich Re): sliding scale commission range
    recorded as 25-35% but treaty states 27-33%.

Impact Assessment:
  Potential misstatement in the counterparty default risk module of
  up to EUR 3,000,000. Within materiality threshold but indicative
  of a control weakness.

Management Response (Frau Elena Vogt, Head of Reinsurance):
  Accepted. Immediate correction of the two discrepancies. Full
  reconciliation of all 12 treaties to be completed by 28 February 2026.

OBSERVATION 3 — MINOR
Title: QRT Submission Timeline Compliance
Rating: MINOR
Risk Area: Solvency II Pillar 3 — Reporting

Description:
  The Q3 2025 Quantitative Reporting Templates (QRTs) were submitted
  to BaFin 2 business days before the regulatory deadline. While
  technically compliant, the Solvency II Reporting Policy (POL-SII-003)
  requires submission 5 business days before the deadline to allow for
  supervisory queries.

Impact Assessment:
  No regulatory breach. Reputational risk only.

Management Response (Herr Klaus Braun, Head of Regulatory Reporting):
  Accepted. Resource constraints in Q3 noted. Additional FTE approved
  for the reporting team effective January 2026.
