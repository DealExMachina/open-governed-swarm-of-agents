1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Following the publication of EIOPA's updated guidelines on the look-
through approach (EIOPA-BoS-26/142, effective 15 April 2026), Meridian
has recalculated the SCR as at 31 March 2026 applying the enhanced
look-through requirements.

The recalculated SCR ratio is 128%, a significant reduction from the
Q4 2025 ratio of 142%. This report details the drivers of the change.

IMPORTANT — BITEMPORAL NOTE:
The Q4 2025 SCR ratio of 142% (Report dated 18 January 2026) remains
the OFFICIAL regulatory figure for the Q4 2025 reporting period. That
calculation was performed in accordance with the guidelines then in
force (EIOPA-BoS-14/171). The recalculation herein is performed under
the NEW guidelines and represents the Q1 2026 position. The Q4 figure
is NOT restated.

5. IMPLICATIONS

5.1 Regulatory Threshold Analysis
  Board minimum:    120%  — NOT breached (8pp buffer)
  Board target:     135%  — BREACHED by 7 percentage points
  Supervisory ladder: No supervisory intervention triggered at 128%

5.2 Transitional Measure Impact
  Per Guideline 4 of EIOPA-BoS-26/142, the change in SCR exceeds
  10% (actual: 8.0%), but the change in SCR ratio exceeds 10pp
  (actual: 14pp). Therefore, BaFin MAY require early recalculation
  of the Art. 308c transitional measure. See separate memo
  (08-transitional-measure-review.txt).

5.3 Retroactive Assessment
  As required by Guideline 3, Meridian will submit the retroactive
  assessment to BaFin by 14 June 2026. This assessment will show
  the hypothetical Q4 2025 SCR under the new guidelines for
  informational purposes only.
