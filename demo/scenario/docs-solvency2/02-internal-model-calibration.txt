1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This report documents the annual calibration review of Meridian's
partial internal model (PIM) as approved by BaFin under Decision
Ref. VA 44-I 4000-2023/0189 dated 15 September 2023. The PIM covers
the life underwriting risk module and market risk module. All other
modules use the standard formula.

2. MODEL PARAMETERS — LIFE UNDERWRITING RISK

2.1 Mortality Risk
  Base mortality table: DAV 2004 T (modified)
  Stress factor (1-in-200 year): 18.5% (standard formula: 15%)
  Pandemic loading: additional 3.2% applied post-COVID recalibration
  Resulting capital charge: EUR 67,000,000

2.2 Lapse Risk
  Mass lapse stress: 42% of in-force (standard formula: 40%)
  Lapse-up stress: 55% increase (standard formula: 50%)
  Lapse-down stress: 67% decrease (standard formula: 65%)
  Resulting capital charge: EUR 89,000,000
  Note: Lapse risk is the dominant sub-module within life underwriting.

2.3 Catastrophe Risk (Life)
  Absolute stress: 1.8 per mille of sum at risk
  (standard formula: 1.5 per mille)
  Resulting capital charge: EUR 42,000,000

3. MODEL PARAMETERS — MARKET RISK

3.1 Interest Rate Risk
  Calibration method: principal component analysis on EUR swap curve
  Stress scenarios: parallel shift +/- 150bps, twist, inversion
  Resulting capital charge: EUR 112,000,000

3.2 Equity Risk
  Type 1 equities stress: 41% (standard formula: 39% + symmetric adj.)
  Type 2 equities stress: 52% (standard formula: 49% + symmetric adj.)
  Resulting capital charge: EUR 94,000,000

3.3 Spread Risk
  Internal model uses migration-based approach
  Calibration period: 2005-2025 (20 years, includes GFC and COVID)
  Resulting capital charge: EUR 83,000,000
