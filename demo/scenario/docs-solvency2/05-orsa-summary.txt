1. PURPOSE

This ORSA summary report is prepared in accordance with Art. 45 of
Directive 2009/138/EC and EIOPA Guidelines on ORSA (EIOPA-BoS-14/259).
It presents Meridian's assessment of its overall solvency needs in
relation to its risk profile, approved risk tolerance limits, and
business strategy over the 3-year planning horizon (2026-2028).

2. CURRENT SOLVENCY POSITION (AS AT 31 DECEMBER 2025)

SCR ratio:            142% (see SCR Baseline Report dated 18 January 2026)
MCR ratio:            412% (MCR = EUR 216,000,000)
Eligible own funds:   EUR 890,000,000
SCR:                  EUR 627,000,000
Surplus:              EUR 263,000,000

The Board's risk appetite statement (approved 12 November 2025) sets:
  - Minimum SCR ratio: 120% (hard floor)
  - Target SCR ratio: 135% (operating range: 135%-160%)
  - Maximum SCR ratio: 170% (above which excess capital to be returned)

4. STRESS SCENARIOS

4.1 Interest Rate Shock (parallel shift +200bps)
  SCR ratio impact: -18 percentage points (142% -> 124%)
  Own funds impact: EUR -72,000,000
  Breach of target? YES (below 135%)
  Breach of minimum? NO

4.2 Pandemic Event (200-year return period)
  SCR ratio impact: -14 percentage points (142% -> 128%)
  Own funds impact: EUR -56,000,000
  Breach of target? YES (below 135%)
  Breach of minimum? NO

4.3 Financial Crisis (combined equity -40%, spreads +250bps)
  SCR ratio impact: -31 percentage points (142% -> 111%)
  Own funds impact: EUR -134,000,000
  Breach of target? YES
  Breach of minimum? YES (below 120%)
  Recovery time to 120%: 18 months (with management actions)

4.4 Mass Lapse (25% of unit-linked portfolio)
  SCR ratio impact: -9 percentage points (142% -> 133%)
  Own funds impact: EUR -38,000,000
  Breach of target? YES (marginal)
  Breach of minimum? NO

4.5 Regulatory Change (loss of transitional measure)
  SCR ratio impact: -7 percentage points (142% -> 135%)
  Own funds impact: EUR 0 (own funds unchanged, SCR recalculated)
  Breach of target? NO (exactly at target)
  Breach of minimum? NO

5. REVERSE STRESS TEST

The reverse stress test identifies the combination of events that
would render Meridian non-viable (SCR ratio below 100%):
  - Scenario: equity markets fall 35%, credit spreads widen 200bps,
    mass lapse of 20%, AND a 1-in-100 year natural catastrophe
  - Combined probability: approximately 1-in-500 years
  - This is beyond the Board's risk tolerance

6. ASSESSMENT OF CONTINUOUS COMPLIANCE

Meridian expects to maintain continuous compliance with SCR and MCR
requirements over the 3-year planning horizon under the base scenario
and all individual stress scenarios except Scenario 4.3 (Financial
Crisis). For Scenario 4.3, the capital contingency plan (approved
12 November 2025) includes:
  - Dividend suspension (EUR 40M-45M annual savings)
  - Reduction of equity allocation (EUR 60M capital release)
  - Subordinated debt issuance (pre-approved facility: EUR 200M)

7. OVERALL SOLVENCY NEEDS

Meridian's overall solvency needs, taking into account risks not
captured by the standard formula (reputational risk, strategic risk,
liquidity risk), are assessed at EUR 690,000,000 — approximately
10% above the regulatory SCR of EUR 627,000,000.

The current eligible own funds of EUR 890,000,000 provide adequate
coverage of overall solvency needs (ratio: 129%).
