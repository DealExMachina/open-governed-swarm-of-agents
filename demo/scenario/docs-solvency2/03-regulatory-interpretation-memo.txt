MERIDIAN INSURANCE GROUP AG
Regulatory Interpretation Memorandum
Subject: Application of Art. 308c Transitional Measures on Technical Provisions
Prepared by: Regulatory Interpreter — Dr. Anna-Lena Richter (Legal & Compliance)
Classification: CONFIDENTIAL — Restricted Distribution
Date of Issue: 28 January 2026
Validity: This interpretation is effective as at 31 December 2025

1. BACKGROUND

Article 308c of Directive 2009/138/EC (Solvency II) permits insurance
and reinsurance undertakings to apply a transitional deduction to
technical provisions, subject to supervisory approval. The transitional
measure is designed to ensure an orderly transition from the Solvency I
regime by allowing a gradual phase-in of the Solvency II technical
provisions over a 16-year period ending 1 January 2032.

2. CURRENT REGULATORY POSITION

2.1 Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/35
  Art. 308c(2): The transitional deduction shall be calculated as the
  difference between Solvency II technical provisions and Solvency I
  technical provisions, reduced linearly over the 16-year period.

2.2 BaFin Circular 2/2016 (VA)
  Paragraphs 18-24 provide guidance on the calculation methodology.
  Key requirement: recalculation every 24 months or upon material
  change in risk profile.

2.3 EIOPA Guidelines on Transitional Measures (EIOPA-BoS-15/116)
  Guideline 5: The transitional deduction applies at the level of
  individual homogeneous risk groups.
  Guideline 7: Changes in interest rates since 1 January 2016 shall
  be reflected in the Solvency I proxy calculation.

3. MERIDIAN'S APPLICATION

Meridian received BaFin approval for the transitional measure on
3 March 2024 (Ref. VA 44-I 4000-2024/0031). The current transitional
deduction is calculated as follows:

  Solvency II technical provisions (gross):  EUR 4,280,000,000
  Solvency I proxy technical provisions:     EUR 4,190,000,000
  Difference:                                EUR    90,000,000
  Linear reduction factor (10/16 remaining):          50.0%
  Transitional deduction:                    EUR    45,000,000

This EUR 45,000,000 deduction reduces Meridian's technical provisions
for SCR calculation purposes.

4. INTERPRETATION ISSUES

4.1 Look-Through and Transitional Interaction
  QUESTION: Should the look-through approach for collective investment
  undertakings (Art. 84, Delegated Regulation) interact with the
  transitional calculation?

  INTERPRETATION: Based on current BaFin guidance and EIOPA Q&A
  ID 1621 (published 14 July 2024), the look-through approach does
  NOT affect the transitional deduction calculation. The transitional
  is computed at the aggregate technical provisions level, whereas
  look-through affects asset-side risk charges. These are independent.

  CONFIDENCE: HIGH — supported by explicit EIOPA Q&A.

4.2 Counterparty Downgrade and Transitional
  QUESTION: If a reinsurance counterparty is downgraded, does this
  affect the transitional deduction?

  INTERPRETATION: No. The transitional measure relates solely to the
  difference between Solvency I and Solvency II technical provisions.
  Counterparty credit quality affects the counterparty default risk
  module, not the technical provisions calculation that underpins
  the transitional.

  CONFIDENCE: HIGH — no ambiguity in the regulation.

4.3 Retroactive Application of Future Guideline Changes
  QUESTION: If EIOPA issues updated guidelines affecting the look-
  through approach, would prior SCR calculations need to be restated?

  INTERPRETATION: Under Art. 308c(4), prior approved calculations
  remain valid until the next scheduled recalculation date unless
  BaFin specifically requires an earlier recalculation. A change in
  EIOPA guidelines does not automatically invalidate previously
  approved figures. However, the NEXT recalculation must incorporate
  any new requirements.

  CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM — while legally sound, supervisory practice
  may differ. BaFin has discretion under Art. 308c(5) to require
  earlier recalculation.

5. RECORD-KEEPING REQUIREMENT

Per Art. 308c(6) and BaFin Circular 2/2016 para. 23, Meridian must
retain complete documentation of each transitional calculation,
including the Solvency I proxy methodology, for a minimum of 5 years
after the transitional period ends (i.e., until 1 January 2037).

This memorandum serves as part of that documentation.
