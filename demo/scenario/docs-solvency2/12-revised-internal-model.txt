1. PURPOSE

This report documents the recalibration of Meridian's partial internal
model (PIM) in response to:
  (a) BaFin's supervisory letter dated 22 May 2026, Section 2.2;
  (b) The enhanced look-through data now available for all 47
      collective investment undertakings; and
  (c) The need to maintain model appropriateness under Art. 120 of
      Directive 2009/138/EC.

2. SCOPE OF RECALIBRATION

The PIM covers the life underwriting risk module and market risk module.
This recalibration focuses on the MARKET RISK MODULE, as the enhanced
look-through data materially affects equity and spread risk parameters.
Life underwriting parameters are unchanged from the January 2026 review.

3. RECALIBRATED PARAMETERS — MARKET RISK

3.1 Equity Risk (MATERIAL CHANGE)
  Previous Type 1 stress: 41% (standard formula: 39%)
  Revised Type 1 stress:  45%

  Previous Type 2 stress: 52% (standard formula: 49%)
  Revised Type 2 stress:  56%

  Reason: Full look-through reveals concentration in technology and
  emerging market equities within funds MSIG-EQ-14 and MSIG-EQ-22.
  These holdings exhibit higher volatility than the broad indices
  previously assumed under the mandate-based approach. Backtesting
  against 2020-2025 data confirms the higher stress is appropriate.

  Revised capital charge:  EUR 118,000,000 (was EUR 94,000,000)
  Increase:                EUR  24,000,000

3.2 Spread Risk (MATERIAL CHANGE)
  The migration-based model has been recalibrated to reflect the
  actual credit quality distribution revealed by look-through:
  - 8.4% of fixed income within funds is rated BBB or below
    (previously assumed < 3% under mandate-based approach)
  - Concentrated exposure to EUR 12,000,000 in high-yield bonds
    within Fund MSIG-FI-03

  Revised capital charge:  EUR 104,000,000 (was EUR 83,000,000)
  Increase:                EUR  21,000,000

3.3 Interest Rate Risk (MINOR CHANGE)
  Revised capital charge:  EUR 115,000,000 (was EUR 112,000,000)
  Increase:                EUR   3,000,000
  (Reflects updated duration profile from look-through bond data)

3.4 Currency Risk (NEW SUB-MODULE)
  Previously immaterial under mandate-based approach. Full look-
  through reveals EUR 28,000,000 in USD-denominated assets and
  EUR 15,000,000 in GBP-denominated assets within funds.

  Capital charge:          EUR  11,000,000 (previously EUR 0)

CRITICAL FINDING:

The recalibrated internal model now produces a HIGHER combined SCR
than the standard formula by EUR 7,000,000 (1.5%). This REVERSES
the previous relationship where the internal model produced a LOWER
SCR by EUR 14,000,000 (3.2%).

Comparison history:
  January 2026 calibration:  Internal model SCR LOWER by 3.2%
  August 2026 calibration:   Internal model SCR HIGHER by 1.5%
  Swing:                     4.7 percentage points

This reversal is primarily driven by:
  - Equity concentration risk captured by internal model but not by
    standard formula (EUR +24M vs +22M standard formula)
  - Currency risk now modeled internally (EUR +11M vs +8M standard)
  - Spread risk migration approach more sensitive to credit quality
    deterioration than standard formula factor-based approach

5. IMPLICATIONS

5.1 Regulatory Reporting
  Under Art. 112 of Directive 2009/138/EC, Meridian must report
  the SCR using the HIGHER of the internal model and standard
  formula results. Previously, this distinction was immaterial as
  both approaches produced similar results.

  Going forward, Meridian will report the PIM SCR for the modules
  covered by the internal model (market risk and life underwriting),
  as this is the approved regulatory basis. The standard formula
  comparison is maintained for validation purposes.

5.2 Capital Remediation Plan Impact
  The remediation plan (CRP-2026-001) was based on standard formula
  figures. If internal model figures are used for the Q3 2026
  calculation, the projected Q3 SCR ratio may be approximately
  2 percentage points lower than projected.

  Revised Q3 2026 projection: approximately 153% (was 155.3%)
  This remains within the target range but with less buffer.

5.3 Model Change Notification
  This recalibration constitutes a MAJOR model change under BaFin's
  classification framework. A formal model change application must
  be submitted to BaFin within 30 days (by 7 September 2026) under
  Art. 115 of Directive 2009/138/EC.
