2. FOLLOW-UP: OBSERVATION 1 — LOOK-THROUGH APPROACH (WAS MATERIAL)

Original Finding: Look-through data not obtained for 7 of 47 funds.
Original Rating: MATERIAL
Target Remediation Date: Q2 2026

Current Status: CLOSED — REMEDIATED

Evidence reviewed:
  (a) Meridian now receives quarterly look-through data from all 47
      fund managers. Contracts with 7 previously non-compliant managers
      were amended to include mandatory quarterly data provision clauses.
  (b) As at 30 June 2026, 100% of fund holdings (EUR 340,000,000)
      are subject to full look-through. The mandate-based approach is
      no longer applied to any fund.
  (c) Data refresh frequency: quarterly, with maximum 25-day lag
      (within the 30-day EIOPA requirement per EIOPA-BoS-26/142).
  (d) Automated data quality checks implemented in the risk system
      (Prophet) to flag missing or stale fund composition data.

Internal Audit is satisfied that this observation has been fully
remediated. The control framework for look-through is now robust.

Note: While this observation is closed from an audit perspective,
the FINANCIAL IMPACT of the original gap (approximately EUR 50,000,000
additional SCR) has been a significant driver of Meridian's current
capital position challenges. The cost of the governance failure
should be noted by the Audit Committee.

5. NEW OBSERVATION 4 — CONTRACT BOUNDARY CLASSIFICATION

Rating: MATERIAL
Risk Area: Solvency II Pillar 1 — Technical Provisions (Best Estimate)
Reference: Art. 18 of Delegated Regulation (EU) 2015/35

5.1 Description

During a thematic review of Meridian's technical provisions calculation
methodology, Internal Audit identified a potential misclassification of
contract boundaries for a portfolio of group life insurance contracts.

Specifically, Meridian's group life portfolio (EUR 420,000,000 in best
estimate liabilities) includes 312 contracts where the policyholder
has a unilateral right to terminate with 3 months' notice, but where
the insurer has a contractual right to adjust premiums at each annual
renewal. Meridian currently projects cash flows to the END of the
contractual term (typically 5 years), treating these as fixed-term
contracts.

Under Art. 18 of the Delegated Regulation, the contract boundary is
the point at which the insurer can unilaterally refuse premiums,
reject claims, or adjust premiums to fully reflect the risk. If
Meridian's annual premium adjustment right is deemed to "fully
reflect the risk," the contract boundary should be set at the NEXT
annual renewal date (not the end of the 5-year term).

5.2 Quantitative Impact Assessment

If contract boundaries are shortened to the next annual renewal:
  Best estimate liabilities reduction: approximately EUR 85,000,000
  Risk margin reduction: approximately EUR 8,000,000
  Total technical provisions reduction: approximately EUR 93,000,000

  HOWEVER, this also:
  - Reduces future premium income projections (lower assets)
  - Changes the lapse risk profile (lower lapse-down stress)
  - Affects the transitional measure calculation (changes the
    Solvency II / Solvency I difference)

  Net SCR impact: estimated EUR -15,000,000 to EUR +10,000,000
  (direction uncertain, depends on interaction effects)

5.3 Regulatory Risk

This is a known area of supervisory scrutiny. EIOPA published a
supervisory statement on contract boundaries (EIOPA-BoS-25/456) in
November 2025, calling for consistent application across Member States.
BaFin has not yet issued national guidance.

If BaFin determines that Meridian's current treatment is incorrect,
a restatement of technical provisions could be required. This would
represent a further contradiction in the solvency position narrative:
the same portfolio would have different values depending on the
contract boundary interpretation applied.

5.4 Management Response (Herr Prof. Stefan Bruckner, Chief Actuary)
  The current treatment is based on legal advice from Clifford Chance
  LLP (Opinion dated 14 March 2024) that the premium adjustment clause
  does NOT "fully reflect the risk" within the meaning of Art. 18.
  However, the Chief Actuary acknowledges that this is a judgment call
  and commits to:
  (a) Engaging BaFin in a proactive dialogue on this topic;
  (b) Preparing parallel calculations under both interpretations for
      the Q3 2026 reporting cycle;
  (c) Updating the ORSA to include a contract boundary stress scenario.

  Target completion: 31 December 2026
