4. LEGAL CONFLICT ANALYSIS

The erasure request creates a direct conflict between two binding legal
obligations:

  GDPR ARTICLE 17(1) — RIGHT TO ERASURE:
  "The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller
   the erasure of personal data concerning him or her without undue delay."

  Grounds cited by requester:
  - Art. 17(1)(a): Data no longer necessary for original purpose
  - Art. 17(1)(c): Data subject withdraws consent

  5AMLD ARTICLE 40 — RECORD RETENTION:
  "Member States shall require obliged entities to retain... copies of
   the documents and information which are necessary to comply with the
   customer due diligence requirements... for a period of five years
   after the end of the business relationship."

  German GwG Section 8(4) — RETENTION:
  "Records referred to in subsections 1 to 3 shall be retained for a
   period of five years... Documents required for CDD shall be retained
   for five years after the end of the business relationship."

  GDPR ARTICLE 17(3)(b) — EXEMPTION:
  "Paragraphs 1 and 2 shall not apply to the extent that processing is
   necessary... for compliance with a legal obligation which requires
   processing by Union or Member State law to which the controller is
   subject."

5. KEY QUESTIONS FOR RESOLUTION

  (a) Has the "business relationship" ended with respect to Elena?
      Elena is no longer a UBO, but the relationship with Castellan
      Holdings continues. Elena's CDD data was collected as part of
      the ongoing client relationship.

  (b) When does the 5-year retention period begin?
      If measured from Elena's cessation as UBO: 2024-07-15 + 5 years
      = 2029-07-15 (earliest possible deletion).
      If measured from end of Castellan Holdings relationship: unknown
      future date + 5 years.

  (c) Is the AML retention obligation a valid Article 17(3)(b) exemption?
      Legal analysis: YES — 5AMLD record retention is a "legal obligation
      which requires processing" and constitutes a valid exemption to
      the right of erasure.

  (d) Can partial erasure be applied?
      Some data (e.g., marketing consents, non-CDD communications) may
      not be subject to AML retention requirements and could be deleted.

8. PRELIMINARY DPO ASSESSMENT

The right to erasure is NOT absolute. The AML/KYC record retention
obligation under 5AMLD and GwG constitutes a valid legal basis for
continued processing under GDPR Article 17(3)(b). However, the bank
must minimize data processing to what is strictly necessary for AML
compliance and must implement appropriate technical and organizational
measures to restrict access to retained data.

A balanced resolution must be found that respects both Elena's data
protection rights and EuroBank's legal obligations.
