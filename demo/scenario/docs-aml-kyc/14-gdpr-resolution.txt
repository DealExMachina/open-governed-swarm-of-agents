1. DECISION SUMMARY

Following comprehensive legal analysis, cross-functional consultation,
and balancing of the competing legal obligations, the Data Protection
Officer issues the following decision:

  REQUEST STATUS: PARTIALLY GRANTED, PARTIALLY DENIED

  Elena Castellan's personal data will be RETAINED where required by
  anti-money laundering legislation, but will be subject to enhanced
  access restrictions. Data not required for AML compliance will be
  DELETED.

2. LEGAL BASIS FOR RETENTION

The DPO relies on the following legal bases for continued processing
of Elena Castellan's personal data:

  Primary Basis: GDPR Article 17(3)(b)
  "The right to erasure shall not apply to the extent that processing
   is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation which requires
   processing by Union or Member State law to which the controller is
   subject."

  Applicable Legal Obligations:
  (a) 5AMLD (Directive 2015/849) Article 40: 5-year retention of CDD
      records after end of business relationship or last transaction
  (b) German GwG Section 8(4): 5-year retention of CDD documentation
  (c) German AO (Abgabenordnung) Section 147: 6-year retention of
      commercially relevant correspondence; 10-year for accounting records
  (d) German HGB Section 257: 10-year retention for certain commercial
      records

  The DPO determines that Elena Castellan's CDD data falls squarely
  within the scope of 5AMLD/GwG mandatory retention. The bank is
  legally prohibited from deleting this data during the retention period.

3. DATA CATEGORIZATION AND DISPOSITION

  Category A — AML/CDD RETAINED DATA (erasure denied):
    - Passport copy and identification documents
    - UBO declaration and beneficial ownership records
    - Source of wealth documentation
    - Tax residency certificates
    - CDD assessment records naming Elena as UBO
    - Risk assessments referencing Elena's ownership stake
    - Transaction records where Elena is identified as connected party
    - Correspondence regarding CDD verification
    - EDD records referencing Elena

    Retention Period: Until 2029-07-15 (5 years from cessation of UBO
    status on 2024-07-15) OR 5 years after end of Castellan Holdings
    relationship, whichever is LATER.

    Legal Basis: GDPR Art. 6(1)(c) (legal obligation) + Art. 17(3)(b)

  Category B — NON-AML DATA TO BE DELETED (erasure granted):
    - Marketing preferences and consent records
    - Event invitation history
    - Personal lifestyle notes in RM file (art interests, travel)
    - Non-essential email correspondence not related to CDD/AML
    - Cookie and website tracking data (EuroBank online portal)

    Deletion Deadline: 2024-10-22 (14 days from decision)

  Category C — DERIVED DATA TO BE ANONYMIZED:
    - Internal risk model training data incorporating Elena's profile
    - Statistical and analytical datasets
    - Management reporting aggregates

    Anonymization Deadline: 2024-10-22

4. ACCESS RESTRICTION MEASURES

For Category A retained data, the following restrictions are implemented
to comply with GDPR data minimization principles (Art. 5(1)(c)):

  (a) Access Control:
    - Elena Castellan's data reclassified to "AML Retention Only"
    - Access restricted to: Head of Financial Crime, DPO, General
      Counsel, assigned KYC analyst, and BaFin/FIU upon request
    - Relationship Manager access REVOKED (no ongoing client need)
    - All access logged with timestamp and purpose justification

  (b) Purpose Limitation:
    - Retained data may ONLY be processed for:
      * AML/KYC regulatory compliance
      * Responding to FIU or BaFin requests
      * Legal proceedings related to the Castellan relationship
      * Internal or external audit of AML compliance
    - Any other processing requires fresh DPO approval

  (c) Technical Measures:
    - Data moved to restricted compliance archive (separate from
      active client file)
    - Encryption at rest (AES-256) with separate key management
    - Automated access alerts to DPO for any retrieval
    - Quarterly access review by DPO

  (d) Retention Schedule:
    - Automated reminder set for 2029-04-15 (90 days before earliest
      possible deletion date) to reassess retention necessity
    - If no ongoing regulatory obligation exists at that time,
      Category A data will be permanently deleted
    - If SAR-related obligations extend the period, retention will
      continue with documented justification

5. COMMUNICATION TO DATA SUBJECT

A formal response has been prepared for Elena Castellan's legal counsel
(Avv. Francesca Vitale, Bonelli Erede):

  Key Points of Response:
  - Acknowledge the request and Elena's rights under GDPR
  - Explain the Art. 17(3)(b) exemption in clear, non-technical language
  - Confirm partial deletion of non-essential data (Category B)
  - Confirm anonymization of derived data (Category C)
  - Explain retention necessity without referencing SAR or investigation
    (tipping-off prohibition)
  - Provide details of access restrictions implemented
  - Inform of right to lodge complaint with competent supervisory
    authority (BfDI — Bundesbeauftragte fuer den Datenschutz)
  - Offer to provide full data inventory upon request (Art. 15 access)

  NOTE: The response must NOT reference the SAR filing, ongoing
  investigation, or BaFin inspection. The retention justification is
  framed solely on the statutory 5AMLD/GwG obligations, which is
  sufficient and accurate without disclosing the additional factors.

  Legal Basis:           Art. 6(1)(c) — legal obligation
  Retention Period:      Until 2029-07-15 (minimum), subject to extension

  DPIA Required:         No (standard regulatory retention, no new risk)

7. PRECEDENT NOTE

This decision establishes internal precedent for handling GDPR erasure
requests from former UBOs or connected parties of active AML clients.
The balanced approach — retain what is legally required, delete what is
not, restrict access to retained data — should be applied consistently.

However, each request must be assessed on its individual merits. The
existence of an active SAR in this case adds additional retention weight
that may not apply in other situations.
