3. JUSTIFICATION FOR MATERIAL CHANGES

  3.1 Transaction Risk (40 → 85):
  EUR 3.3M in payments to Cerberus Consulting AG — a newly incorporated
  entity with shell characteristics, controlled by an undisclosed former
  business partner. Payments lack credible commercial rationale. SAR
  filed (SAR-2024-CAST-0001). Payments represent 63% of annual turnover
  baseline, entirely outside expected patterns.

  3.2 PEP Proximity (35 → 90):
  Marco Castellan (PEP) added as trust beneficiary without disclosure.
  Marco now under active investigation by Guardia di Finanza. Direct
  financial benefit to PEP through trust structure. FATF Recommendation
  12 requires EDD for all business relationships with PEP family members
  where the PEP has a financial interest.

  3.3 Source of Wealth Transparency (25 → 65):
  Original source of wealth documentation remains valid for historical
  assets. However, the undisclosed BVI ownership change (30% to Cerberus)
  and the opaque consulting arrangement raise questions about current
  fund flows and the true economic substance of inter-group transactions.

  3.4 Adverse Media (10 → 80):
  Active adverse media coverage of Marco Castellan's investigation.
  Potential for media coverage to extend to Viktor Castellan and
  Castellan Holdings as the investigation develops.

8. LESSONS LEARNED

The Castellan case has identified the following process improvements
for EuroBank's AML/KYC framework:

  (a) Trust beneficiary disclosure should be a hard requirement at
      onboarding — not accepted as "follow-up" item
  (b) PEP family association scoring should be more conservative when
      combined with complex multi-jurisdictional structures
  (c) New counterparty thresholds should consider cumulative exposure,
      not just single-transaction amounts
  (d) Annual review cycle is insufficient for clients with trust
      structures — semi-annual minimum recommended
  (e) GDPR/AML conflict resolution procedures should be pre-documented
      rather than handled ad hoc

These findings have been submitted to the AML Policy Review Committee
for incorporation into EuroBank Policy Manual v3.2 (expected Q2 2025).
