3. KEY AREAS OF REGULATORY CONCERN (ANTICIPATED)

Based on pre-inspection conversations with BaFin liaison:

  (a) TIMELINESS OF EDD TRIGGER
  BaFin may question why EDD was not triggered EARLIER:
    - Marco Castellan was identified as PEP-adjacent at onboarding
      (March 2023) but only as "low-level association"
    - Trust beneficiary disclosure was requested at onboarding but
      follow-up was not escalated until annual review (April 2024)
    - EDD was formally triggered in July 2024 — 16 months after
      onboarding, 9 months after trust amendment
    - BaFin guidance (BaFin Journal, March 2022) states that undisclosed
      trust beneficiaries should trigger EDD "without delay"

  (b) RISK RATING ADEQUACY
  The initial MEDIUM risk rating may be scrutinized:
    - BVI entity + Liechtenstein trust + undisclosed beneficiaries
      arguably warranted HIGH risk from the start
    - The PEP family association, even at LOW level, combined with
      the BVI/trust complexity could justify EDD at onboarding
    - EuroBank's risk matrix may be challenged as insufficiently
      conservative for this client profile

  (c) TRANSACTION MONITORING THRESHOLDS
  BaFin may question whether monitoring thresholds were appropriate:
    - The EUR 600K single-transaction alert threshold permitted
      a EUR 500K quarterly dividend without alerts
    - The new counterparty threshold (EUR 100K) caught the Cerberus
      payments but only after the initial EUR 1.8M was already sent

  (d) RECORD INTEGRITY
  BaFin will verify that historical records are maintained intact:
    - Original MEDIUM risk rating must be preserved as contemporaneous
    - Subsequent HIGH rating must be a separate dated record
    - All CDD versions must show clear version history
    - No retroactive amendments to historical documents

4. EuroBANK RESPONSE PREPARATION

  4.1 Documentation Package Assembled:
    Complete chronological CDD file with version control — 847 pages
    Transaction monitoring data export — 12,400 transaction records
    Alert disposition records — 6 alerts (3 closed, 3 open)
    SAR file — restricted access, delivered under separate cover
    Risk rating audit trail — 2 versions (MEDIUM → HIGH)

  4.2 Key Defense Points:
    - CDD at onboarding was thorough and compliant with 5AMLD
    - Initial MEDIUM risk rating was a reasonable professional judgment
      given the information available at that time
    - EDD was triggered promptly upon discovery of material changes
    - SAR was filed within 30 days of Compliance Committee decision
    - Transaction monitoring system detected the anomaly correctly
    - Record retention and version control are demonstrably intact

  4.3 Acknowledged Weaknesses:
    - Trust beneficiary follow-up could have been more aggressive
    - Annual review cycle may be insufficient for MEDIUM-risk clients
      with undisclosed trust structures
    - Client notification obligations could be more rigorously enforced
