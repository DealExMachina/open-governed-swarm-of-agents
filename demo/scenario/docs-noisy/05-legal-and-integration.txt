PART 1: LEGAL FINDINGS

This section summarizes findings from the legal due diligence workstream. Full legal memoranda have been provided separately under attorney-client privilege.

1.1 CORPORATE STRUCTURE AND GOVERNANCE

NovaTech AG is a Swiss Aktiengesellschaft domiciled in Zurich with wholly-owned subsidiaries in Germany (NovaTech Deutschland GmbH) and the Netherlands (NovaTech Benelux B.V.). Corporate governance appears standard for a company of this size, with a 5-person board including 2 independent directors. Shareholder agreements contain standard drag-along provisions and a change-of-control clause that would require supermajority (75%) approval for the acquisition.

Minor concerns: the articles of association contain an anti-dilution provision that could complicate post-close equity incentive programs for retained employees. This is a negotiation point, not a blocker.

1.2 INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY DISPUTE -- DETAILED ASSESSMENT

The co-inventorship claim by Dr. Klaus Haber is more complex than initially represented by management. Our review of the relevant correspondence, court filings, and internal communications reveals the following:

Dr. Haber served as a technical advisor to NovaTech from March 2021 to September 2023. His advisory agreement contained an IP assignment clause, but the clause was drafted narrowly and applies only to inventions "directly resulting from advisory services rendered under this agreement." Dr. Haber's claim is that two of the seven patents (EP3847291 and EP3901445) incorporate ideas he developed independently prior to the advisory engagement and contributed during informal brainstorming sessions that were not formally documented as advisory services.

The legal position is genuinely uncertain. Management's characterization of this as "resolved" is inaccurate -- the proceedings are in a preliminary phase with the Swiss Federal Patent Court, and no ruling or settlement has been reached. Our external patent counsel estimates the probability of Haber prevailing on at least one of the two patents at approximately 35-45%, based on the available evidence.

If Haber prevails on both patents, the remediation cost could range from EUR 2M (licensing arrangement) to EUR 8M (redesign of affected subsystems to work around the disputed claims). If he prevails on one, costs would be approximately half that. We recommend establishing an escrow of EUR 4M to cover the contingent liability.

1.3 REGULATORY AND COMPLIANCE STATUS

SOC 2 Type II certification is current and valid through March 2026. GDPR compliance appears adequate based on our review, though we note that the company's data processing agreements with two clients are outdated and reference pre-Schrems II standard contractual clauses. This is a minor compliance gap that can be remediated at low cost.

The EU MDR compliance timeline is the more pressing concern. The July 2026 deadline for updated medical device software requirements will require certification of the cold-chain compliance module under the new framework. NovaTech has not yet initiated the certification process, and the typical timeline for initial certification is 9-12 months. This creates a real risk of a compliance gap in H2 2026, which could result in certain clients being unable to use the cold-chain module for regulated activities until certification is obtained.

PART 2: INTEGRATION ASSESSMENT

2.1 TECHNOLOGY INTEGRATION

Integration of the PCO platform with our existing distribution management system will require development of a bidirectional API layer. Our preliminary technical assessment estimates 4-6 months of development effort, assuming cooperation from NovaTech's engineering team. The assumption of team cooperation is, given the talent risks identified elsewhere, non-trivial.

The architectural compatibility is generally good: both systems use PostgreSQL-based data stores and event-driven architectures, and the API contracts are well-documented. The primary integration challenge will be reconciling the compliance event taxonomies between the two systems, which use different classification schemes.

2.2 ORGANIZATIONAL INTEGRATION

NovaTech's 87 employees (as of September 2025) are distributed across engineering (42), sales and marketing (18), customer success (12), G&A (10), and executive (5). Anticipated headcount reductions from synergies: 8-12 positions in G&A and sales overlaps. Estimated restructuring cost: EUR 1.5M to EUR 2M.

Cultural integration risk is moderate. NovaTech has a startup-oriented culture with significant autonomy for engineering teams. Our integration playbook calls for a 12-month "light touch" integration approach to preserve team cohesion, transitioning to full integration of shared services (HR, Finance, Legal) in year two.

2.3 SYNERGY REALIZATION

Identified synergies total approximately EUR 12M to EUR 18M annually at full run-rate (year 3+):
- Revenue synergies from cross-sell: EUR 8-12M (dependent on successful technical integration)
- Cost synergies from shared infrastructure and G&A: EUR 4-6M

We note that the revenue synergy estimate assumes successful retention of the engineering team and completion of the technical integration. If the talent risks materialize, the revenue synergy timeline could extend by 12-18 months.

PART 3: CONSOLIDATED RISK MATRIX AND RECOMMENDATION

Consolidating findings across all workstreams:

HIGH RISK: ARR overstatement (EUR 8-12M adjustment), talent concentration (3 key departures imminent), IP dispute (35-45% probability of adverse outcome)

MEDIUM RISK: Compliance remediation cost (EUR 2-3.5M unbudgeted), competitive pressure from Axion and Nexion, customer retention (10-15% at risk), EU MDR certification timeline

LOW RISK: Corporate governance, GDPR compliance, integration architectural compatibility

RECOMMENDED REVISED OFFER

Based on adjusted ARR of EUR 38-42M, gross margin of 64-68%, and factoring in identified mitigation costs (retention EUR 1.5-2M, compliance EUR 2.5-3.5M, IP escrow EUR 4M, integration EUR 1.5-2M), the supportable offer range is:

Base case: EUR 290M to EUR 320M (7.0x-7.5x adjusted ARR)
Upside case: EUR 340M to EUR 370M (if talent retained and IP resolved favorably)
Downside case: EUR 240M to EUR 270M (if key departures occur and IP adverse)

This represents a significant reduction from the initial indicative range of EUR 400-430M. We recommend presenting a structured offer with milestone-based earnout provisions tied to talent retention and compliance certification milestones.
