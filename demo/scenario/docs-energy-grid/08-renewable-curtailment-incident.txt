INCIDENT DATE: February 18, 2026

EVENT SUMMARY

On February 18, 2026, sustained high winds across the North and Coastal
balancing areas drove aggregate wind generation to 3,100 MW — exceeding
the Q1 forecast maximum of 2,800 MW by 300 MW (10.7% above forecast
peak capacity factor). Coinciding with low overnight demand (system load
5,200 MW), curtailment was required to maintain system stability.

CURTAILMENT DISPATCH

At 02:15 UTC, NorthGrid dispatch issued curtailment orders to the
following wind farms via the Automatic Generation Control (AGC) system:

  Wind Farm           | Installed | Pre-Curtail | Curtailed To | Reduction
  --------------------|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------
  Ridgeline II        |   280 MW  |    265 MW   |    145 MW    |   120 MW
  Ridgeline III       |   320 MW  |    298 MW   |    148 MW    |   150 MW
  Northern Gale       |   250 MW  |    241 MW   |    101 MW    |   140 MW
  Atlantic Breeze I   |   350 MW  |    332 MW   |    142 MW    |   190 MW
  Prairie Wind        |   220 MW  |    211 MW   |     11 MW    |   200 MW
  --------------------|-----------|-------------|--------------|----------
  TOTAL               | 1,420 MW  |  1,347 MW   |    547 MW    |   800 MW

Curtailment duration: 02:15 UTC to 09:45 UTC (7.5 hours)
Total curtailed energy: approximately 6,000 MWh

SETTLEMENT SYSTEM DISCREPANCY

The settlement system (using SCADA telemetry at 15-minute intervals)
recorded a total curtailment of only 500 MW — a discrepancy of 300 MW
from the actual dispatched curtailment of 800 MW.

Investigation reveals the following root cause:

  1. Settlement metering relies on 15-minute interval reads from revenue
     meters at the point of interconnection.

  2. During the curtailment period, wind speed was highly variable
     (gusting between 12 m/s and 22 m/s), causing the available wind
     generation to fluctuate significantly within each 15-minute window.

  3. The settlement system calculates curtailment as:
       Curtailment = (Available Generation) - (Metered Output)
     where "Available Generation" is estimated from a 15-minute average
     wind speed applied to the manufacturer's power curve.

  4. Because the 15-minute averaged wind speed understates peak gusts
     and overstates lull periods, the estimated "available generation"
     was systematically lower than actual available generation.

  5. Actual available generation, measured by the farms' own 4-second
     SCADA systems, showed consistently higher output capability than
     the 15-minute average implied.

FINANCIAL IMPACT

  Curtailment compensation methodology:
    Settlement system calculation:  500 MW x 7.5 hrs x €35/MWh = €131,250
    Actual curtailment (metered):   800 MW x 7.5 hrs x €35/MWh = €210,000
    Underpayment to wind farms:     €78,750 for this single event

  Annualized impact if methodology not corrected:
    Estimated 12-18 similar events per year
    Potential cumulative underpayment: €0.9M - €2.1M annually

Wind farm operators are requesting an independent metering review and
methodology correction for all curtailment events in Q1 2026.

PRELIMINARY ASSESSMENT

The 15-minute telemetry sampling rate is inadequate for accurate
curtailment settlement during variable wind conditions. The 4-second
data from the wind farms' own SCADA systems provides materially
different — and more accurate — curtailment volumes.

This issue was flagged in the December 2025 settlement report
(NG-SETL-2025-12-005) as a known limitation. Project NG-PROJ-2026-017
(telemetry upgrade to 4-second settlement metering) is pending budget
approval.
