CURTAILMENT FORECAST

High-wind/low-demand curtailment events expected: 12-18 days in Q1

Curtailment risk periods identified:
  - Overnight hours (00:00-06:00) when wind generation exceeds 90% of
    installed capacity AND system load drops below 5.5 GW
  - Estimated curtailment volume: 180-260 GWh (7-10% of wind generation)
  - Primary constraint: North-to-Central transmission corridor (2,400 MW
    thermal limit on the Ridgeline-Lakeside 345kV path)

Economic impact of forecast curtailment: €8.2M - €12.4M in lost
production tax credits and renewable energy certificates.

RAMP RATE CONCERNS

Wind ramp events exceeding 500 MW/hour expected: 22-30 events in Q1
Wind ramp events exceeding 1,000 MW/hour: 4-6 events (severe)

Recommended mitigation:
  - Maintain 400 MW of fast-ramping gas peaker capacity on standby during
    high-wind forecast periods
  - Utilize 150 MW battery storage at Lakeside for ramp smoothing
  - Coordinate with MidAtlantic ISO for emergency energy exchange during
    severe down-ramp events

INTERCONNECTION QUEUE

Pending renewable projects in NorthGrid interconnection queue:
  - 3 wind farms:  total 1,200 MW (expected COD: 2027-2028)
  - 5 solar farms: total 800 MW (expected COD: 2027)
  - 2 battery storage: total 300 MW/600 MWh (expected COD: 2027)

Grid impact studies for these projects are underway. Preliminary results
indicate North-Central corridor reinforcement required before additional
wind capacity can be reliably integrated.
