SYSTEM OVERVIEW

NorthGrid Utilities operates as a Regional Transmission Operator (RTO)
serving approximately 6.2 million customers across a four-state service
territory. The bulk electric system (BES) encompasses 12.4 GW of peak
generating capacity distributed across four balancing areas.

BALANCING AREAS

  Area       | Nodes | Peak Cap. | Primary Generation
  -----------|-------|-----------|----------------------------
  North      |  214  |  3.1 GW   | Nuclear (Millstone 1&2), Wind
  Central    |  287  |  4.2 GW   | Natural gas (combined cycle)
  South      |  198  |  2.8 GW   | Gas peakers, Solar farms
  Coastal    |  148  |  2.3 GW   | Offshore wind, Gas turbines

Total transmission nodes: 847
Total transmission line-miles: 14,200 (345kV: 3,800; 230kV: 5,100; 115kV: 5,300)

INTERCONNECTIONS

NorthGrid maintains AC tie-lines with three neighboring ISOs:
  1. MidAtlantic ISO — 4 x 345kV ties, 2,800 MW transfer capacity
  2. Great Lakes ISO — 2 x 345kV ties, 1,400 MW transfer capacity
  3. Southeast Pool  — 3 x 230kV ties, 1,100 MW transfer capacity

Total import capability:  5,300 MW (firm), 6,800 MW (emergency)
Total export capability:  4,200 MW (firm), 5,500 MW (emergency)

CRITICAL SUBSTATIONS

The following substations are classified as Critical Facilities under
NERC CIP-014 (physical security):
  - Granite Falls 345kV Switching Station
  - Harborview 345/230kV Autotransformer Station
  - River Bend Nuclear Switchyard
  - Lakeside Central Hub (5-bus, 345kV)

SYSTEM CONDITION

All four balancing areas operating within NERC reliability standards as
of this baseline date. N-1 contingency analysis shows no thermal
overloads. Voltage profiles within +/-5% of nominal at all monitored
buses. Phase angle differences across tie-lines within stability limits.

Next topology review scheduled: April 2026 (post-winter assessment).
