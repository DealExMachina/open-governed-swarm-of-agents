PURPOSE

This document presents the detailed frequency analysis of the February 12
grid disturbance using high-resolution Phasor Measurement Unit (PMU) data
and compares findings against the SCADA-based readings reported in the
initial event report (NG-EVENT-2026-02-12-006).

MEASUREMENT COMPARISON

The initial event report cited a SCADA-recorded frequency nadir of
59.87 Hz at 16:48:23 UTC. PMU data analysis reveals a significantly
different picture of the sub-second transient behavior.

  Source  | Sample Rate | Nadir (Hz) | Nadir Time (UTC)     | Duration <59.95
  --------|-------------|------------|----------------------|-----------------
  SCADA   | 0.5 Hz      | 59.87      | 16:48:23             | 47 sec
  PMU-01  | 30 Hz       | 59.823     | 16:48:22.467         | 53 sec
  PMU-02  | 30 Hz       | 59.818     | 16:48:22.500         | 54 sec
  PMU-03  | 30 Hz       | 59.826     | 16:48:22.433         | 52 sec
  PMU-04  | 30 Hz       | 59.821     | 16:48:22.500         | 54 sec
  PMU-05  | 30 Hz       | 59.819     | 16:48:22.467         | 53 sec

PMU consensus nadir: 59.82 Hz (+/- 0.004 Hz across 5 PMUs)
SCADA reported nadir: 59.87 Hz

DISCREPANCY: 0.05 Hz (50 mHz)

ROOT CAUSE OF MEASUREMENT DISCREPANCY

The 50 mHz discrepancy between SCADA and PMU readings is attributable to
SCADA's 2-second averaging window. During the frequency transient:

  1. The actual frequency trajectory showed a sharp decline over
     approximately 1.2 seconds, reaching a nadir of 59.82 Hz that
     persisted for only 0.8 seconds before primary frequency response
     began restoring frequency.

  2. SCADA's 2-second scan captured an averaged value across the entire
     decline-and-partial-recovery cycle, reporting 59.87 Hz — which
     represents neither the true nadir nor the post-response value.

  3. The 5 PMUs, sampling at 30 Hz (33ms intervals), captured the full
     transient waveform with sub-cycle resolution. All 5 PMUs agree
     within a 0.008 Hz band (59.818 to 59.826 Hz).

IMPLICATIONS FOR COMPLIANCE REPORTING

The revised nadir of 59.82 Hz is materially different from the
SCADA-based 59.87 Hz reported initially:

  1. BAL-003 exceedance severity increases — the event penetrated
     deeper into the under-frequency range than initially reported.
  2. The 59.82 Hz nadir was only 0.07 Hz above the UFLS Stage 1
     trip setting of 59.75 Hz — a margin of 70 mHz rather than the
     120 mHz margin implied by the SCADA reading.
  3. NERC must be notified of the corrected nadir value.

RECOMMENDATION

  1. Accept PMU data as ground truth for this event. PMU measurement
     uncertainty (+/-0.005 Hz) is an order of magnitude better than
     SCADA's effective resolution during transients.
  2. File corrected OE-417 supplement with revised nadir of 59.82 Hz.
  3. Revise NorthGrid Procedure NG-OP-301 to designate PMU data as
     the primary source for frequency compliance reporting during
     disturbance events, superseding SCADA.
  4. Do NOT overwrite original SCADA-based reports. Annotate them
     as "superseded" with reference to PMU-corrected values.
