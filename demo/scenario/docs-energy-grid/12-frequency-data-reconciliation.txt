PURPOSE

This document formalizes the reconciliation between PMU and SCADA
frequency measurements for the February 12 Winter Storm Helios event
and establishes the authoritative record for NERC compliance reporting.

RECONCILIATION DECISION

After review by the Measurement Accuracy Review Board (MARB) on
March 7, 2026 (MARB session 2026-04), the following determination
was reached:

  PMU data is accepted as ground truth for the February 12 event.

  Authoritative frequency nadir: 59.82 Hz (+/- 0.005 Hz)
  Timestamp: February 12, 2026, 16:48:22.500 UTC
  Duration below 59.95 Hz: 53 seconds (PMU) vs. 47 seconds (SCADA)

  Previous SCADA-based value: 59.87 Hz — SUPERSEDED

RATIONALE

The MARB determination is based on the following technical analysis:

  1. Measurement resolution: PMU sampling at 30 Hz provides 60x the
     temporal resolution of SCADA's 0.5 Hz effective scan rate. During
     sub-second transient events, SCADA's 2-second averaging window
     introduces systematic measurement error by smoothing the true
     frequency trajectory.

  2. Consistency: Five independent PMUs at geographically diverse
     locations agreed within a 0.008 Hz band (59.818 - 59.826 Hz),
     demonstrating high measurement confidence. SCADA measurements
     from different locations showed a wider spread of 0.02 Hz,
     consistent with their lower resolution.

  3. Standards compliance: IEEE C37.118.1-2011 (Synchrophasor
     Measurement for Power Systems) specifies Total Vector Error (TVE)
     requirements of less than 1% for PMUs. NorthGrid's PMU fleet
     was last calibrated in November 2025, all within specification.

  4. Physical consistency: The PMU-recorded frequency trajectory is
     consistent with the known generation loss (3,200 MW), system
     inertia constant (H = 4.2 seconds), and governor response
     characteristics. The SCADA value of 59.87 Hz is NOT consistent
     with these physical parameters — it implies an unrealistically
     fast primary frequency response.

DATA MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL

The following data governance actions have been implemented:

  1. ORIGINAL SCADA RECORDS PRESERVED
     The original SCADA-based event report (NG-EVENT-2026-02-12-006)
     and January frequency report (NG-FREQ-2026-01-003) are NOT
     overwritten, deleted, or modified. They remain in the document
     management system with their original content intact.

  2. ANNOTATION APPLIED
     Each superseded document has been annotated with the following
     metadata tag in the NorthGrid Document Control System (DCS):

       STATUS: SUPERSEDED
       SUPERSEDED BY: NG-FREQ-2026-03-RECON-012
       REASON: PMU ground truth reconciliation — frequency nadir
               revised from 59.87 Hz (SCADA) to 59.82 Hz (PMU)
       DATE: March 10, 2026
       AUTHORITY: MARB Session 2026-04

  3. FORWARD-LOOKING PROCEDURE CHANGE
     NorthGrid Procedure NG-OP-301 (Frequency Measurement and Reporting)
     has been revised (Rev. 4, effective March 15, 2026):
       - PMU data designated as primary source for frequency compliance
         reporting during disturbance events (NERC Category 1 and above)
       - SCADA data remains primary source for routine (non-event)
         frequency monitoring
       - Discrepancy threshold of 0.02 Hz triggers automatic PMU review

  - Original filing: frequency nadir 59.87 Hz, duration 47 seconds
  - Corrected filing: frequency nadir 59.82 Hz, duration 53 seconds

NERC ReliabilityFirst acknowledged receipt on March 9, 2026 and
confirmed the corrected values will be used for compliance evaluation.

IMPLICATIONS FOR BAL-003 COMPLIANCE

The revised nadir of 59.82 Hz increases the severity of the BAL-003
exceedance assessment:
  - SCADA-based margin to UFLS Stage 1 (59.75 Hz): 120 mHz
  - PMU-based margin to UFLS Stage 1 (59.75 Hz):    70 mHz

The reduced margin (70 mHz vs. 120 mHz) may influence the NERC
enforcement disposition. NorthGrid's legal team has been briefed.
