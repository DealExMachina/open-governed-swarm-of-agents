SUBJECT: TransCon Energy LLC — Pipeline Line 4200 Supply Failure
         During Winter Storm Helios (February 12-14, 2026)

INVESTIGATION INITIATED: February 15, 2026
FERC DOCKET: RP26-412-000 (Preliminary Investigation)

BACKGROUND

During Winter Storm Helios, NorthGrid experienced a loss of 3,200 MW of
gas-fired generation, the primary cause being reduced gas supply pressure
on TransCon Energy's Pipeline Line 4200, which serves NorthGrid's Central
and South balancing area gas generation fleet.

TransCon Energy filed a force majeure declaration on February 13, 2026
(TransCon ref: FM-2026-0087), claiming that "unprecedented cold weather
conditions caused pipeline pressure drops beyond operational control."

NorthGrid's investigation challenges this characterization.

TRANSCON ENERGY'S STATED POSITION

Per force majeure declaration FM-2026-0087:
  - "Extreme cold caused compressor station efficiency losses of 15-20%"
  - "Pipeline demand across all customers exceeded design capacity"
  - "Gas supply was allocated pro rata to all firm customers"
  - "NorthGrid received its contractual minimum firm delivery volume"
  - "Conditions were unforeseeable and beyond TransCon's control"

NORTHGRID'S EVIDENCE (CONTRADICTING FORCE MAJEURE)

NorthGrid's gas nomination and flow monitoring systems recorded the
following sequence of events on Pipeline Line 4200:

  Date/Time (UTC)   | Flow to NorthGrid | Pipeline Pressure | Note
  -------------------|-------------------|-------------------|--------
  Feb 11, 23:00      | 1,850 MMcf/d      | 812 psig          | Normal
  Feb 12, 02:00      | 1,840 MMcf/d      | 805 psig          | Normal
  Feb 12, 05:00      | 1,620 MMcf/d      | 748 psig          | REDUCED
  Feb 12, 08:00      | 1,380 MMcf/d      | 691 psig          | ALERT
  Feb 12, 11:00      | 1,100 MMcf/d      | 623 psig          | CRITICAL
  Feb 12, 14:00      |   780 MMcf/d      | 542 psig          | EMERGENCY

Key finding: Gas flow to NorthGrid began declining at 05:00 UTC on
February 12 — six hours BEFORE the storm reached peak intensity and
BEFORE ambient temperatures dropped below the -10C threshold that
TransCon cites as the cause of compressor efficiency losses.

At 05:00 UTC, ambient temperature was -6C, well within normal winter
operating range for TransCon's compressor stations (rated to -15C).

CONTRADICTORY EVIDENCE: INDUSTRIAL CUSTOMER FLOW DATA

NorthGrid obtained (via FERC discovery request) TransCon's allocation
records for February 12, 2026. These records show:

  Customer               | Contract  | Feb 12 Actual | Feb 11 Actual
  -----------------------|-----------|---------------|---------------
  NorthGrid Utilities    | 1,850 Firm| 1,100 avg     | 1,840
  ChemCorp Industries    |   600 IT  |   850         |   590
  SteelWorks National    |   400 IT  |   620         |   410
  Regional Gas Dist. Co. | 1,200 Firm|   980         | 1,180

NOTE: "IT" = Interruptible Transportation, "Firm" = Firm Transportation

Critical observation: ChemCorp Industries and SteelWorks National, both
holding INTERRUPTIBLE transportation contracts, received INCREASED gas
flows on February 12 compared to February 11 — while NorthGrid's FIRM
transportation was being curtailed.

Total interruptible customer flow increase: +460 MMcf/d
NorthGrid firm customer flow decrease:     -750 MMcf/d

Under FERC regulations (18 CFR 284.7) and TransCon's FERC Gas Tariff,
interruptible transportation must be curtailed BEFORE firm transportation
during capacity constraints.

FINANCIAL ANALYSIS

NorthGrid's estimated damages from the gas supply failure:
  - Emergency power purchases:           €8.2M
  - Demand response activation costs:    €4.6M
  - Gas spot market premium purchases:   €3.1M
  - Generation equipment damage (CT2):   €1.8M
  - Customer claims and goodwill:        €2.4M
  - NERC penalty exposure (BAL-003):     €0.5M - €2.0M (estimated)
  ----------------------------------------
  Total estimated damages:               €20.6M - €22.1M

REGULATORY FILINGS

  1. FERC complaint filed February 26, 2026 (Docket RP26-412-000)
     alleging TransCon violated its tariff by prioritizing interruptible
     customers over firm customers during a capacity constraint.
  2. FERC requesting TransCon produce all allocation records,
     compressor station logs, and customer communications for Feb 10-15.
  3. NorthGrid has engaged outside counsel (Baker & McKenzie) for
     potential civil litigation if FERC resolution is insufficient.

INTERIM ACTIONS

  1. NorthGrid has diversified gas supply: new firm contract with
     PeakGas Pipeline (Line 5500) for 400 MMcf/d backup supply.
  2. Dual-fuel capability assessment underway for Central area gas units.
  3. TransCon placed on NorthGrid's Enhanced Supplier Monitoring list.
