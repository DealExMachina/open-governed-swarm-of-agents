BACKGROUND

Four wind farm operators filed formal settlement disputes on February
19-20, 2026, challenging the curtailment compensation calculations for
the February 18, 2026 curtailment event. Total disputed amount: EUR 78,750
across four disputes. The wind farms contended that the settlement system
understated actual curtailment by 300 MW (recording 500 MW vs. actual
800 MW dispatched curtailment).

INDEPENDENT METERING REVIEW

The Dispute Resolution Panel retained GridMetrics LLC (independent
metering consultant) to perform a forensic analysis of the February 18
curtailment event. GridMetrics analyzed data from three independent
sources:

  Source 1: NorthGrid SCADA (15-minute interval settlement telemetry)
  Source 2: Wind farm SCADA (4-second interval operational telemetry)
  Source 3: Revenue meter pulse accumulator data (1-minute resolution)

FINDINGS

GridMetrics Report (GM-2026-NG-018, dated March 8, 2026):

  Data Source          | Curtailment Volume | Avg. MW Curtailed
  ---------------------|--------------------|-----------------
  NorthGrid 15-min     |    3,750 MWh       |    500 MW
  Revenue meter 1-min  |    5,640 MWh       |    752 MW
  Wind farm 4-sec      |    6,012 MWh       |    802 MW

The independent review confirms that actual curtailment was approximately
800 MW, consistent with the dispatch instruction issued by NorthGrid's
control room at 02:15 UTC.

ROOT CAUSE OF SETTLEMENT DISCREPANCY

GridMetrics identified the following technical chain of error:

  1. AVAILABLE GENERATION ESTIMATION
     The settlement system estimates available (uncurtailed) wind
     generation using a static power curve model applied to 15-minute
     average wind speed data from met masts at each wind farm.

  2. TEMPORAL AVERAGING EFFECT
     During the curtailment period (02:15 - 09:45 UTC), wind speeds
     were highly variable. The 15-minute averaging:
       - Understated peak wind speeds by 8-15% (gusts smoothed out)
       - Overstated lull periods by 5-10%
       - Net effect: available generation underestimated by ~300 MW

  3. POWER CURVE NON-LINEARITY
     Wind power is proportional to wind speed cubed (P ~ v^3). This
     means averaging wind speed before applying the power curve yields
     a LOWER result than averaging power output directly.

     Example from Ridgeline III (15-minute window 03:00-03:15):
       15-min avg wind speed: 14.2 m/s --> power curve output: 248 MW
       4-sec power outputs averaged: 298 MW
       Difference: 50 MW (20% understatement)

  4. QUANTIFIED IMPACT
     Across all 5 curtailed wind farms over 7.5 hours:
       15-min method: total available = 4,250 MWh, output = 3,750 MWh,
                      curtailment = 500 MW avg (3,750 MWh)
       4-sec method:  total available = 9,762 MWh, output = 3,750 MWh,
                      curtailment = 802 MW avg (6,012 MWh)

PANEL DETERMINATION

The Dispute Resolution Panel rules in favor of the wind farm operators
on all four disputes. Determinations:

  1. The 4-second wind farm SCADA data provides materially more accurate
     curtailment measurement than the 15-minute settlement telemetry.

  2. The settlement system's 15-minute averaging methodology introduces
     a systematic negative bias in curtailment calculation during
     variable wind conditions.

  3. Total additional compensation owed to wind farm operators:

     Operator                | Original  | Corrected | Additional Due
     ------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------------
     Ridgeline Wind Partners |  €70,875  | €141,750  |   €70,875

     ------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------------
     TOTAL                   | €210,000  | €420,000  |  €210,000

  4. The Panel further orders NorthGrid to:
     a. Review all Q1 2026 curtailment events for similar methodology
        errors and issue corrective settlements where applicable.
     b. Estimated total Q1 correction: EUR 2.1M across 14 events.

SETTLEMENT METHODOLOGY REFORM

The Panel recommends NorthGrid implement the following reforms:

  1. IMMEDIATE (by April 15, 2026): Use 4-second wind farm SCADA data
     as the primary source for curtailment volume calculation, replacing
     the 15-minute averaged methodology.

  2. SHORT-TERM (by Q3 2026): Complete deployment of 4-second telemetry
     settlement metering at all renewable generation points of
     interconnection (Project NG-PROJ-2026-017, now elevated to
     mandatory status).

  3. METHODOLOGY: Replace the "wind speed to power curve" estimation
     with direct measurement of available power via the wind farm's
     own available power signal (per IEC 61400-26-2).
