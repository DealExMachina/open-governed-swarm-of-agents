AUDIT SCOPE

This report documents the quarterly evidence coarsening process whereby
NorthGrid's real-time operational data is aggregated into compliance
metrics for the Q1 2026 NERC filing. The audit examines the fidelity
and completeness of the aggregation pipeline and addresses auditor
questions raised during the Q4 2025 external audit.

COARSENING METHODOLOGY

The evidence coarsening pipeline operates in four stages:

Stage 1 — RAW COLLECTION (millisecond to second resolution)
  All operational data systems continuously write to local historians.
  PMU data: 30 samples/second per channel, 12 PMUs, 8 channels each.
  SCADA data: 2-second scan rate, 847 monitored points.
  Cybersecurity logs: event-driven, variable rate.

Stage 2 — INTERMEDIATE AGGREGATION (minute to hourly resolution)
  Automated jobs aggregate raw data into intermediate summaries:
    - Frequency: 1-minute averages, maxima, minima, std. deviation
    - Generation: 5-minute energy intervals, 15-minute meter reads
    - Cyber logs: hourly event counts by severity and asset
    - Physical security: hourly alarm summaries by perimeter

Stage 3 — DAILY ROLLS (daily resolution)
  Daily compliance calculations executed at 00:15 UTC:
    - BAL-001 CPS1/CPS2 daily components
    - BAL-003 frequency response metrics
    - CIP event counts and compliance status flags
    - Settlement energy balances and imbalance calculations

Stage 4 — QUARTERLY SYNTHESIS (quarterly compliance metrics)
  Final aggregation into 847 NERC-reportable metrics:
    - Weighted compliance index (WCI) per standard
    - Reliability performance metrics (CPS1, CPS2, BAAL)
    - BES Cyber Asset compliance status per requirement
    - Physical security compliance status per perimeter
    - Event narratives and corrective action summaries

AUDITOR QUESTIONS AND RESPONSES

QUESTION 1: Are sub-second frequency transients preserved or lost
during coarsening?

RESPONSE: Sub-second transients are preserved in the PMU archive at
full resolution (30 samples/second) for 13 months from the date of
recording. After 13 months, PMU data is coarsened to 1-minute averages
(min, max, mean, std. dev.) and retained for 7 years per NorthGrid
Data Retention Policy NG-DRP-2024-001.

For the February 12 Storm Helios event specifically:
  - Full 30 Hz PMU data: retained through March 2027
  - After coarsening: the 59.82 Hz nadir would be preserved in the
    1-minute minimum field, but the sub-second trajectory would be lost
  - The 0.8-second duration of the nadir would not survive coarsening

DEMONSTRATION: NorthGrid provided the auditor with a live query of the
OSIsoft PI historian, retrieving 30 Hz PMU data for the February 12
event window (16:48:00 - 16:50:00 UTC). All 3,600 data points per PMU
were retrieved successfully, confirming archive integrity.

QUESTION 2: How is the coarsening process validated for accuracy?

RESPONSE: Three validation mechanisms are in place:

  1. AUTOMATED RECONCILIATION: Daily automated checks compare Stage 2
     aggregated values against random samples of Stage 1 raw data.
     Tolerance: +/- 0.1% for energy quantities, +/- 0.005 Hz for
     frequency values. Q1 pass rate: 99.94% (52 of 87,600 checks
     flagged, all resolved as rounding differences).

  2. EVENT SPOT-CHECKS: For each NERC-reportable event, a manual
     comparison of raw data vs. compliance metric is performed.
     Q1: 3 reportable events, all spot-checked, 1 discrepancy found
     (the SCADA vs. PMU frequency nadir — resolved per document
     NG-FREQ-2026-03-RECON-012).

  3. ANNUAL EXTERNAL AUDIT: Deloitte & Touche performs an annual
     review of the coarsening pipeline (last audit: October 2025,
     next audit: October 2026). No material findings in last audit.

QUESTION 3: What is the risk of material information loss?

RESPONSE: The primary risk is loss of sub-second transient information
after the 13-month PMU retention window. For the February 12 event:

  Information preserved after coarsening:
    - Frequency nadir value (59.82 Hz) — preserved in 1-min minimum
    - Duration below threshold (53 seconds) — preserved in event log
    - Total energy imbalance — preserved in settlement data

  Information lost after coarsening:
    - Sub-second frequency trajectory (0.8-sec nadir shape)
    - Rate of Change of Frequency (RoCoF) waveform
    - PMU phase angle dynamics during the transient
    - Inter-area oscillation modes present during recovery

  Risk assessment: LOW for compliance purposes (the nadir value and
  duration are preserved). MEDIUM for future event analysis (the
  detailed transient shape is lost, which could be valuable for
  validating generator model parameters or system inertia studies).

MULTI-RESOLUTION TEMPORAL INTEGRITY

The audit confirms NorthGrid maintains data at four temporal resolutions
simultaneously during Q1 2026:

  Resolution    | Data Type                | Volume      | Retention
  --------------|--------------------------|-------------|----------
  Millisecond   | PMU synchrophasor        | 2.1B pts    | 13 months
  Second        | SCADA operational        | 389M pts    | 3 years
  Minute/Hourly | Intermediate aggregates  | 6.5M pts    | 7 years
  Daily/Quarter | Compliance metrics       | 847 metrics | Permanent

The temporal tower operates correctly: higher-resolution data feeds
into lower-resolution aggregations, with each level independently
queryable and auditable.

KNOWN LIMITATION

The settlement metering temporal gap (15-minute reads vs. 4-second
actuals) creates a known coarsening artifact in curtailment calculations.
This was the root cause of the February 18 settlement dispute
(NG-SETL-2026-03-DISP-013). The planned upgrade to 4-second settlement
telemetry (NG-PROJ-2026-017) will close this gap by Q3 2026.

AUDIT OPINION

The evidence coarsening pipeline is functioning as designed, with
appropriate controls and validation. The 13-month full-resolution PMU
retention is adequate for current compliance requirements but should be
reviewed if NERC introduces sub-second reporting obligations (currently
under NERC Project 2024-05 consideration).

The single material discrepancy found this quarter (SCADA vs. PMU
frequency nadir) was identified through the existing spot-check process
and corrected through the established data reconciliation procedure.
This demonstrates the control framework is effective.
