EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NorthGrid Utilities submits this quarterly compliance filing covering
NERC CIP standards CIP-002 through CIP-014 and NERC Reliability
Standards BAL-001, BAL-003, and FAC-001 through FAC-014.

Q1 2026 was a challenging quarter marked by Winter Storm Helios
(February 12-14), a self-reported CIP-007 patch management violation,
and a physical security incident during the storm. The weighted
compliance index (WCI) declined from 94% to 91%.

NEW FINDINGS THIS QUARTER

FINDING NF-2026-Q1-01: CIP-007-6 R2 Patch Management Failure
  Severity: Medium-High
  Self-reported: February 24, 2026 (RF-SR-2026-NG-0042)
  Description: 34 of 247 BES Cyber Assets (13.8%) failed to receive
  February security patches within the mandatory 35-day window.
  Includes 3 high-impact relay protection IEDs.
  Root cause: Vendor compatibility issue compounded by Winter Storm
  Helios maintenance freeze.
  Remediation: 31 of 34 assets patched by February 28. Remaining 3
  relay IEDs patched March 12 during scheduled outage windows.
  Status: CLOSED (remediated). Corrective Action Plan submitted.

FINDING NF-2026-Q1-02: CIP-006-6 R1 Physical Security Incident
  Severity: Medium
  Self-reported: March 2, 2026 (RF-SR-2026-NG-0047)
  Description: During Winter Storm Helios, physical security perimeter
  at Harborview 345/230kV station was breached when storm damage
  disabled 2 of 4 perimeter intrusion detection sensors and 1 access
  control card reader. Physical security was degraded for approximately
  18 hours (Feb 12, 14:00 UTC to Feb 13, 08:00 UTC).
  Mitigating actions during event: Armed security guard posted at
  disabled access point within 45 minutes of detection. CCTV coverage
  maintained throughout.
  Root cause: Sensor and card reader rated to -15C but exposed to
  -18C with wind chill -28C.
  Remediation: Equipment upgraded to arctic-rated models (-40C).
  Status: CLOSED (remediated). Equipment replacement complete March 18.
