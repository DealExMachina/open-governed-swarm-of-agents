EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Following the Winter Storm Helios event (February 12-14, 2026), the
NorthGrid Board of Directors has approved a comprehensive grid resilience
remediation plan. The plan addresses the four primary failure modes
identified in the event investigation: gas supply dependency, insufficient
energy storage, weather forecasting gaps, and measurement system
limitations.

PROGRAM 1: BATTERY ENERGY STORAGE SYSTEM (BESS)
Investment: EUR 22M | Timeline: 12 months to COD

Scope:
  - 200 MW / 400 MWh lithium iron phosphate (LFP) BESS installation
  - Location: Lakeside Central Hub (Central balancing area)
  - Grid services: frequency response (primary and secondary),
    spinning reserve, renewable curtailment absorption

Performance requirements:
  - Full power response within 200 milliseconds of frequency trigger
  - 2-hour discharge at rated capacity (400 MWh)
  - Round-trip efficiency >= 88%
  - Operational temperature range: -25C to +45C

Procurement: RFP issued March 1, 2026 (NG-PROC-2026-BESS-001)
  Shortlisted vendors: Tesla Megapack, BYD Cube, Fluence Gridstack
  Contract award target: April 30, 2026
  Construction start: June 2026
  Commercial operation date: March 2027

Impact on system resilience:
  - Provides 200 MW of fast frequency response (replaces gas governor)
  - 400 MWh can cover 30 minutes of 800 MW generation shortfall
  - Reduces minimum gas dependency by 200 MW during extreme cold events

PROGRAM 2: DUAL-FUEL CAPABILITY RETROFIT
Investment: EUR 15M | Timeline: 18 months

Scope:
  - Retrofit 4 Central area gas combined-cycle units with No. 2 fuel oil
    backup capability:
      Lakeside CC Unit 1 (400 MW) — dual-fuel conversion
      Lakeside CC Unit 2 (400 MW) — dual-fuel conversion
      Lakeside CC Unit 3 (400 MW) — dual-fuel conversion
      Flatlands CT Unit 1 (150 MW) — dual-fuel conversion
  - On-site fuel oil storage: 5-day supply at full output (42,000 barrels
    per unit, total 168,000 barrels)

Engineering:
  - Combustion system modification (new fuel nozzles, fuel forwarding)
  - Environmental permits required (USEPA Title V amendment)
  - Air quality permit modifications (NOx, SOx, PM emission limits)

Timeline:
  - Engineering design: March - June 2026
  - Environmental permitting: April - September 2026
  - Construction (phased): October 2026 - August 2027
  - Full capability: September 2027

Impact on system resilience:
  - 1,350 MW of gas generation can operate independently of gas pipeline
  - Eliminates single-point-of-failure dependency on TransCon Pipeline

PROGRAM 3: ENHANCED WEATHER FORECASTING INTEGRATION
Investment: EUR 3M | Timeline: 6 months

Scope:
  - Integration of ensemble weather forecasting models into dispatch
    optimization system
  - Automated pre-positioning of reserves based on probabilistic severe
    weather forecasts (72-hour and 168-hour horizons)
  - Partnership with NOAA/NWS for enhanced regional mesoscale modeling
  - Machine learning model for gas supply risk scoring based on weather,
    pipeline conditions, and historical patterns

Deliverables:
  - Automated severe weather playbook activation (replaces manual process)
  - Pipeline gas supply risk dashboard (real-time)
  - Enhanced wind and solar forecasting (target: 15% MAPE reduction)

Timeline:
  - Vendor selection: March 2026
  - Development and integration: April - July 2026
  - Testing and validation: August 2026
  - Operational deployment: September 2026

PROGRAM 4: MEASUREMENT SYSTEM UPGRADE
Investment: EUR 5M | Timeline: 9 months

Scope:
  - Expand PMU deployment from 12 to 36 units across all critical
    substations (all CIP-014 facilities and major generation POIs)
  - Upgrade SCADA frequency measurement from 2-second to 100ms scan rate
  - Implement PMU-based real-time frequency monitoring as primary source
    (replacing SCADA as primary for disturbance analysis)
  - Deploy synchrophasor-based oscillation detection and early warning

Timeline:
  - PMU procurement: March - April 2026
  - Installation phase 1 (12 units, high-impact sites): May - July 2026
  - Installation phase 2 (12 units, remaining critical): Aug - Oct 2026
  - SCADA upgrade and integration: November 2026
  - Full operational capability: December 2026

FUNDING AND COST RECOVERY

Total program cost: EUR 45M
Funding sources:
  - Rate base investment (FERC-jurisdictional): EUR 32M
  - Insurance recovery (storm damage): EUR 4M
  - TransCon litigation settlement (anticipated): EUR 5M
  - Operational savings reallocation: EUR 4M

Rate impact: approximately EUR 0.42/MWh surcharge for 36 months
(subject to FERC approval under Formula Rate, Attachment H)
