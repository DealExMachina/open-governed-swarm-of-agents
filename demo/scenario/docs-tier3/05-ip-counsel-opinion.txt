EXTERNAL IP COUNSEL OPINION — Project Horizon Core Module
Date: Week 5

Re: IP title for [REDACTED] software module.

Summary opinion: Title is PROBABLY valid but NOT CERTAIN. The chain of
assignment from the original developer to the current holding entity contains
one undated assignment agreement. Under German law (applicable jurisdiction),
undated assignments are not automatically invalid, but they create a rebuttable
presumption of dispute in contested proceedings.

The third-party claimant's position: The claimant asserts that the undated
assignment post-dates their own license agreement, which (if true) would give
them a co-ownership claim. We have not been able to independently verify the
date of the license agreement.

Our assessment:
- Probability that title is fully clear and uncontested at closing: 65%
- Probability that title requires remediation (escrow or indemnity): 30%
- Probability that title is materially defective: 5%

IMPORTANT: This is a probabilistic assessment, not a legal opinion of title.
We cannot provide a definitive title opinion on the current documentation.
A clean title opinion would require either: (a) a dated and notarized version of
the assignment agreement, or (b) a waiver from the third-party claimant.

Neither document has been obtained.

Implication for risk assessment: The IP risk cannot be scored deterministically.
The range remains: 5% chance of material defect (deal-blocking), 30% chance of
remediation cost (€0.5M–€2M), 65% chance of clean outcome.

This opinion DOES NOT resolve the contradiction in prior assessments. It
replaces binary certainty with a probability distribution. Governance decision
required on whether to proceed under this residual uncertainty.
