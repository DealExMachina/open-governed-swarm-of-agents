PROJECT ATLAS — CYBERSECURITY ASSESSMENT

Prepared by: InfoSec Advisory Team
Date: 2025-10-08
Classification: STRICTLY CONFIDENTIAL

CRITICAL FINDINGS

The cybersecurity assessment reveals material concerns not identified in the initial review:

1. INCIDENT HISTORY: MedCore experienced a data breach in March 2024 affecting 12,000 patient records across 3 hospital networks. This was not disclosed in the initial assessment which claimed 99.95% uptime SLA achievement. The breach resulted in 72 hours of service degradation.

2. VULNERABILITY ASSESSMENT: Penetration testing identified 4 critical and 11 high-severity vulnerabilities in the platform's API layer. Two critical vulnerabilities allow unauthenticated access to patient data endpoints.

3. COMPLIANCE GAP: The ISO 13485 certification is current but the most recent audit (June 2024) was conducted prior to a major platform rewrite. The certification may not cover the current production codebase.

4. ENCRYPTION: Data at rest is encrypted with AES-128, below the healthcare industry standard of AES-256. Data in transit uses TLS 1.2, which should be upgraded to TLS 1.3.

These findings materially contradict the initial assessment's characterization of the cybersecurity posture and SLA achievement.

RISK RATING: HIGH

Remediation cost estimated at EUR 3-5M over 12 months.
