1. PURPOSE

This document presents the final risk assessment for the proposed acquisition
of NovaTech AG at EUR 280 million. It supersedes the preliminary risk
assessment (Document 10) and incorporates all findings from Phase 2 and
Phase 3 diligence.

3. DETAILED RISK QUANTIFICATION

  3.1 Financial Performance — HIGH
  Initial:   ARR EUR 50.0M, 45% CAGR, 72% margins, clean
  Final:     ARR EUR 38.0M (-24%), 32% CAGR, 68.4% margins, material misstatement
  Residual:  Revenue recognition practices require monitoring post-close.
             CFO Stefan Gruber's credibility diminished. Consider CFO replacement.
  Quantified Exposure: EUR 12.0M overstatement already priced into revised offer.
             Ongoing risk of further restatement: EUR 2-5M (10% probability)

  3.2 Intellectual Property — MEDIUM (reduced from HIGH pre-settlement)
  Initial:   Clean portfolio, 7 patents, FTO opinion clear
  Final:     2 patents disputed, 1 infringement suit filed, settlements pending
  Residual:  If settlements execute per framework (Document 22), IP risk reduces
             to MEDIUM. Axion royalty obligation creates ongoing cost. EP3847291
             ownership now secured. EP3512644 co-ownership resolved.
  Quantified Exposure: Settlement cost EUR 3.4-4.1M (budgeted). Residual
             litigation risk: EUR 2-5M (5% probability of settlement failure).
             EUR 25M escrow covers worst-case.

  3.3 Customer Concentration — HIGH
  Initial:   Top client 15.6% of ARR, 47 clients, 96% retention
  Final:     Top client 21.6% of adjusted ARR, Pharmakon renewal at -15%,
             performance exit clause, top-3 = 44.5% of ARR
  Residual:  Pharmakon churn risk remains (est. 30% probability over 2 years).
             Revenue impact if Pharmakon churns: EUR 7.0M ARR loss.
  Quantified Exposure: Expected loss = EUR 7.0M x 30% = EUR 2.1M annual risk.
             Earn-out structure partially mitigates (EUR 30M contingent).

  3.4 Technology & Product — HIGH
  Initial:   Modern stack, 12% tech debt, >80% coverage, 99.92% uptime
  Final:     54% actual test coverage, 14-month DR gap, 3 critical CVEs,
             AWS single-source dependency, OpenAI data residency concern
  Residual:  Post-remediation (EUR 5-7M budget), tech risk reduces to MEDIUM.
             Critical CVEs must be resolved pre-signing (Board Condition 3).
  Quantified Exposure: Remediation budget EUR 5-7M. Data breach risk: EUR
             10-20M (2% probability, insurable). DR failure risk: EUR 3-5M
             (10% probability of extended outage before remediation).

  3.5 Regulatory & Compliance — MEDIUM
  Initial:   SOC 2 clean, ISO 27001, GDPR compliant, no pending matters
  Final:     FINMA gap, Swiss data transfer violations, MDR reclassification,
             SOC 2 reliability questioned, German severance understatement
  Residual:  FINMA and data transfer issues are remediable (6 months, EUR 1.5M).
             MDR compliance is a strategic investment (EUR 4.5M, 18 months).
             German severance delta (EUR 2.3M) priced into revised offer.
  Quantified Exposure: Regulatory fines: EUR 0.5-1.0M (15% probability).
             MDR non-compliance market access risk: EUR 3-6M annual revenue
             at risk (customers in clinical-adjacent use cases).

  3.6 Team & Key Person — HIGH
  Initial:   Founders committed, low churn, VP Eng under retention agreement
  Final:     CTO departing Q4 2025, 2 senior engineers following, 61% code
             concentration, ML team flight risk (8 of 18 may leave)
  Residual:  CTO transition plan required (Board Condition 2). EUR 15M
             retention pool allocated. Even with mitigation, 12-18 month
             period of elevated execution risk during leadership transition.
  Quantified Exposure: Full team replacement cost: EUR 15-25M. With retention
             pool and transition plan: EUR 5-10M expected cost. Productivity
             loss during transition: 15-25% reduction in engineering velocity
             for 12 months.

5. FINAL RECOMMENDATION

Despite the significant increase in risk profile from the preliminary
assessment, the Investment Committee recommends PROCEEDING with the
acquisition at EUR 280M, subject to the three Board conditions (Document 24).

The deal remains strategically compelling. The EUR 2.1B addressable market,
NovaTech's product differentiation, and the MDR compliance opportunity
justify the investment. The revised price (EUR 280M vs. EUR 420M) adequately
reflects discovered risks, and the escrow/earn-out structure provides
downside protection.

Risk Rating: MEDIUM-HIGH — PROCEED WITH CONDITIONS
