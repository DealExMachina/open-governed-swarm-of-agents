1. LAWSUIT FILING

On May 8, 2025, Axion Corp GmbH ("Axion"), a Munich-based regulatory technology
company, filed a patent infringement action against NovaTech AG in the Unified
Patent Court (UPC), Munich Local Division (Case No. UPC_CFI_2025/004891).

2. CLAIMS ASSERTED

Axion asserts infringement of European Patent EP4102883 ("AI-Driven Regulatory
Risk Assessment Framework"), granted March 15, 2025. Axion claims that
NovaTech's Predictive Risk Scoring module (covered by NovaTech's EP3847291)
infringes claims 1-4 and 7-9 of EP4102883.

Specifically, Axion alleges:
  (a) NovaTech's use of ensemble ML models for regulatory risk scoring
      infringes Axion's method claims (claims 1-4)
  (b) NovaTech's automated submission prioritization feature infringes
      Axion's system claims (claims 7-9)
  (c) Infringement has been willful since at least September 2024, when
      Axion's patent application was published

3. RELIEF SOUGHT

  - Damages of EUR 15 million (based on alleged lost profits and royalties)
  - Preliminary injunction preventing NovaTech from offering the Predictive
    Risk Scoring module in all UPC contracting states
  - Permanent injunction and recall of infringing products
  - Court costs and attorney fees

4. CONNECTION TO DR. KLAUS HABER

Court filings reveal that Dr. Klaus Haber is listed as a technical consultant
to Axion Corp in this matter. This confirms the concern raised in Document 12
regarding Axion's interest in EP3847291. It appears Dr. Haber may have provided
Axion with detailed knowledge of NovaTech's implementation of the Predictive
Risk Scoring module.

NovaTech's separation agreement with Dr. Haber includes a 6-month non-compete
clause, which expired September 2024. The non-solicitation clause (12 months)
expired March 2025. Dr. Haber's current engagement with Axion therefore does
not breach his contractual obligations, though it raises questions about
potential misuse of NovaTech trade secrets.

5. IMPACT ON EP3847291 OWNERSHIP

The existing co-ownership dispute regarding EP3847291 (Document 12) is now
further complicated by the Axion lawsuit. If Dr. Haber's ownership claim to
EP3847291 is upheld, NovaTech's ability to assert EP3847291 as a defense
(prior art or cross-license) would be compromised.

6. PRELIMINARY LEGAL ASSESSMENT

  Likelihood of Axion prevailing on infringement:    40-55%
  Likelihood of preliminary injunction granted:      25-35%
  Estimated legal costs (through trial):             EUR 2.0-3.5M
  Potential settlement range:                        EUR 1.5-5.0M

  If a preliminary injunction is granted, NovaTech would need to disable the
  Predictive Risk Scoring module across all EU markets. This module is used
  by 31 of 47 customers and generates approximately EUR 6.0M in annual
  revenue (based on adjusted ARR).

7. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS TAKEN

  (a) Litigation hold notice issued to all NovaTech employees
  (b) External patent litigation counsel retained (Falk & Weber IP)
  (c) Preservation of all communications with Dr. Klaus Haber
  (d) Technical team preparing non-infringement analysis and design-around
      options

8. IMPACT ON ACQUISITION

This lawsuit represents a material development that must be disclosed to
Meridian Capital Partners. The litigation risk, combined with the Haber IP
disputes, creates potential exposure of EUR 15-25M in the worst case and
will likely impact the transaction timeline and valuation.
