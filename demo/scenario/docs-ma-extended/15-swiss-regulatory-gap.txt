1. SCOPE

Walder Wyss AG was engaged to independently verify NovaTech AG's regulatory
compliance status across Swiss, EU, and German jurisdictions. This report
identifies material gaps not disclosed in the initial compliance assessment
(Document 07).

2. CRITICAL FINDING: FINMA COMPLIANCE GAP

Contrary to NovaTech's assertion in Document 07 that "FINMA oversight is not
applicable as NovaTech does not provide financial services," our analysis
reveals that NovaTech's RegAssure platform includes a module ("ComplianceScore")
that generates automated risk assessments used by clients for regulatory
submission prioritization.

Under FINMA Circular 2018/3 (Outsourcing — Banks and Insurers), NovaTech's
services to three Swiss financial-sector clients (including Helvetia
Diagnostics, which is a subsidiary of Helvetia Group, a FINMA-regulated
entity) constitute outsourced compliance functions subject to FINMA oversight.

Specifically:
  - NovaTech has not registered as a critical outsourcing provider with FINMA
  - Required FINMA audit rights clauses are missing from contracts with
    regulated clients
  - Business continuity requirements per FINMA Circular 2023/1 are not met
  - No FINMA-compliant incident reporting procedures are in place

3. CROSS-BORDER DATA TRANSFER VIOLATIONS

Document 07 states that cross-border transfers rely on the "EU-Swiss Data
Protection Adequacy Decision." However, our review found:

  (a) NovaTech transfers personal data of Swiss-domiciled individuals to
      its AWS eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) infrastructure. While the EU-Swiss
      adequacy framework covers EU-to-Swiss transfers, Swiss-to-EU transfers
      of non-EU personal data require separate legal basis under the revised
      Swiss FADP (nDSG).

  (b) NovaTech's Data Processing Agreements with Swiss clients do not include
      the updated Standard Contractual Clauses required under the nDSG since
      September 2023. Fourteen (14) of 22 Swiss-related DPAs reference the
      superseded clauses.

  (c) Three client contracts authorize data processing in "EU/EEA" without
      specifying Switzerland as a separate jurisdiction, creating ambiguity
      under Swiss data protection law.

4. GERMAN SUBSIDIARY — ADDITIONAL FINDINGS

Our review of NovaTech GmbH (Munich) identified:

  (a) The Munich office has 64 employees but has not established a formal
      Betriebsrat (works council), despite exceeding the 5-employee threshold
      under BetrVG Section 1. While formation is employee-initiated, the
      absence creates risk in a change-of-control scenario where employees
      may demand consultation rights.

  (b) Severance liability estimates provided by management (EUR 1.8M) do not
      account for German statutory requirements under the Kuendigungsschutzgesetz
      for employees with >2 years tenure. Our estimate is EUR 4.1M — an
      understatement of EUR 2.3M.

5. REMEDIATION TIMELINE

  FINMA Compliance:
    - Registration and contract amendments: 3-4 months
    - Audit rights and BCP implementation: 4-6 months
    - Total estimated cost: EUR 800K - 1.2M

  Data Transfer Compliance:
    - DPA updates and SCC migration: 2-3 months
    - Estimated cost: EUR 200K - 350K

  Total remediation timeline: approximately 6 months
  Total estimated cost: EUR 1.0M - 1.55M

6. RISK ASSESSMENT

The FINMA gap presents moderate regulatory risk. While no enforcement action
is currently pending, FINMA has increased scrutiny of outsourcing arrangements
since 2024. Discovery during post-acquisition integration could result in
penalties of CHF 100K - 500K and mandatory operational changes.

The data transfer issues present lower but non-trivial risk under the nDSG,
with potential fines up to CHF 250K for individuals and reputational harm.
