1. BACKGROUND

The initial regulatory assessment (Document 07) concluded that NovaTech's
RegAssure platform is "out-of-scope" under EU MDR 2017/745, with caveats
regarding the Predictive Risk Scoring module under Annex VIII Rule 11.

Following the Axion patent dispute (Document 19) and deeper regulatory
analysis during Phase 2 diligence, TUV SUD has revised its assessment.

2. REVISED MDR CLASSIFICATION

TUV SUD now classifies the following RegAssure modules as potentially within
scope of EU MDR 2017/745:

  (a) Predictive Risk Scoring Module — Class IIa medical device software
      Rationale: The module generates risk scores that directly influence
      regulatory submission prioritization decisions for medical devices.
      When used in combination with clinical data, this constitutes
      "software intended to provide information used to take decisions
      with diagnosis or therapeutic purposes" per Rule 11.

  (b) Post-Market Surveillance Analytics — Class I medical device software
      Rationale: The automated trend detection features generate signals
      that may constitute "monitoring of physiological processes" when
      processing vigilance data per MDR Article 83.

  (c) Regulatory Submission Drafting (AI-Generated) — Requires assessment
      Rationale: If AI-generated regulatory submissions are relied upon
      without material human review, the software may be classified as
      providing "information that constitutes a diagnosis" per MDCG 2019-11.

3. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

To achieve full EU MDR compliance for the affected modules, NovaTech must:

  Phase 1 — Quality Management System (Months 1-6):
    - Implement ISO 13485:2016 quality management system
    - Establish clinical evaluation process per MDR Article 61
    - Appoint Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance (PRRC)
    - Develop technical documentation per MDR Annex II and III
    Estimated Cost: EUR 1.2 million

  Phase 2 — Conformity Assessment (Months 6-12):
    - Engage Notified Body for conformity assessment (TUV SUD nominated)
    - Conduct clinical evaluation and performance testing
    - Prepare risk management documentation per ISO 14971
    - Implement post-market surveillance system per MDR Article 83-86
    Estimated Cost: EUR 1.8 million

  Phase 3 — Certification & Market Access (Months 12-18):
    - Obtain CE marking under MDR
    - Register in EUDAMED database
    - Implement Unique Device Identification (UDI) system
    - Establish vigilance reporting capability
    Estimated Cost: EUR 1.5 million

4. TOTAL INVESTMENT

6. RISK OF NON-COMPLIANCE

If NovaTech does not pursue MDR compliance:
  - Risk of enforcement action by competent authorities (BfArM in Germany,
    Swissmedic in Switzerland)
  - Customers using affected modules in clinical contexts may face their own
    regulatory non-compliance
  - Potential market access restrictions in EU member states
  - Reputational damage and loss of customer trust

The revised MDR classification contradicts Document 07's conclusion that
RegAssure is entirely "out-of-scope." This change in classification is a
material finding that impacts both operational costs and competitive
positioning.

7. STRATEGIC CONSIDERATION

Achieving MDR compliance, while costly, would differentiate NovaTech from
competitors (Veeva, Rimsys) that do not hold medical device software
certification. This could become a significant competitive advantage for
customers requiring MDR-compliant tooling in clinical-adjacent use cases.
