1. SCOPE OF ENGAGEMENT

Deloitte AG was engaged by Meridian Capital Partners to perform an independent
Quality of Earnings (QoE) review of NovaTech AG for the period FY2023 through
Q3 FY2025. This report summarizes material findings that deviate from
management-reported figures.

2. CRITICAL FINDING: REVENUE OVERSTATEMENT

Our review identified material discrepancies in NovaTech's reported Annual
Recurring Revenue (ARR). Management reported ARR of EUR 50.0 million as of
Q3 2025. Our adjusted figure is EUR 38.0 million, representing an overstatement
of EUR 12.0 million (24.0%).

The overstatement arises from three sources:

  (a) Deferred Revenue Misclassification (EUR 6.2M):
      NovaTech recognized EUR 6.2 million in multi-year contract revenue
      upfront rather than ratably over the contract term. Specifically,
      three contracts signed in Q2-Q3 2025 with 3-year terms were recognized
      at full annual value in the signing quarter. Under ASC 606 / IFRS 15,
      this revenue should be deferred and recognized over 36 months.

  (b) Professional Services Bundling (EUR 3.1M):
      Implementation and customization revenue totaling EUR 3.1 million was
      classified as recurring subscription revenue. These are one-time
      professional services fees that should be excluded from ARR calculations.

  (c) Churned / At-Risk Revenue Inclusion (EUR 2.7M):
      Two accounts totaling EUR 2.7 million in annual contract value had
      submitted formal non-renewal notices prior to the reporting date.
      These should have been excluded from the ARR figure as of the
      measurement date.

4. ADDITIONAL OBSERVATIONS

  (a) Accounts receivable aging shows EUR 3.2M in invoices >90 days overdue,
      suggesting collection challenges not disclosed by management.

  (b) Related-party transactions: EUR 420K in consulting payments to Haber
      Consulting GmbH (owned by Dr. Klaus Haber, former employee) were not
      disclosed in the management-prepared financials.

  (c) The reported operating loss of EUR 4.3M should be adjusted to EUR 7.8M
      when revenue adjustments and understated expenses are included.

5. IMPACT ON VALUATION

At the adjusted ARR of EUR 38.0M, the indicative offer of EUR 420M represents
an implied multiple of 11.1x ARR — significantly above the comparable
transaction range of 6-12x for companies with NovaTech's adjusted growth
profile (~32% CAGR rather than the reported 45%).

6. RECOMMENDATION

We recommend that Meridian Capital Partners:
  (a) Revise the indicative valuation downward to reflect adjusted metrics
  (b) Request management explanation for all identified discrepancies
  (c) Engage forensic accounting review if discrepancies are not satisfactorily
      explained

This report constitutes a material adverse finding under the diligence protocol.
