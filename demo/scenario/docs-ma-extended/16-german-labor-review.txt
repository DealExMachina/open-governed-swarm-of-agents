1. SCOPE

Hengeler Mueller was engaged to review employment law compliance and labor
liabilities for NovaTech GmbH, the German subsidiary of NovaTech AG, in
connection with the proposed acquisition by Meridian Capital Partners.

  Employees with >5 years tenure:    14 (22%)

3. WORKS COUNCIL (BETRIEBSRAT) STATUS

NovaTech GmbH does not currently have a works council (Betriebsrat). Under
the Betriebsverfassungsgesetz (BetrVG), establishments with five or more
permanent employees have the right to form a works council. While formation
is employee-initiated, the employer cannot obstruct the process.

RISK: In a change-of-control transaction, employees are significantly more
likely to exercise their right to form a Betriebsrat. Once established, the
works council must be consulted on:
  - Any workforce restructuring or layoffs (Section 111 BetrVG)
  - Changes to working conditions, hours, or compensation
  - Transfer of undertaking matters (Section 613a BGB)
  - Social plan negotiations (potentially mandatory)

The acquisition will likely trigger Section 613a BGB (Transfer of Undertaking)
obligations, requiring written notification to all employees at least one
month before closing. Employees have the right to object to the transfer
within one month of notification.

4. SEVERANCE LIABILITY ANALYSIS

Management has estimated severance liabilities at EUR 1.8 million for the
Munich office. Our independent analysis yields a significantly higher figure:

  Statutory severance (KSchG, Section 1a):
    41 employees x average 3.1 years x average monthly salary EUR 7,200
    x 0.5 factor = EUR 456,000 (statutory minimum)

  Negotiated severance (social plan scenario):
    Historical German tech sector social plans average 1.0-1.5 monthly
    salaries per year of service:
    41 employees x 3.1 years x EUR 7,200 x 1.25 = EUR 1,139,400

  Executive / senior management enhanced severance:
    4 senior managers with contractual severance of 6-12 months:
    Estimated EUR 860,000 - EUR 1,200,000

  Fixed-term contract acceleration:
    12 employees with average 14 months remaining:
    Estimated EUR 750,000 - EUR 900,000

  TOTAL ESTIMATED SEVERANCE LIABILITY: EUR 3.2M - EUR 4.1M
  MANAGEMENT ESTIMATE: EUR 1.8M
  UNDERSTATEMENT: EUR 1.4M - EUR 2.3M

5. CHANGE-OF-CONTROL PROVISIONS

Four (4) Munich-based senior employees have change-of-control clauses in their
employment contracts that trigger enhanced severance (12 months base salary)
and accelerated equity vesting upon acquisition. These provisions were not
disclosed in the management-prepared documents.

6. NON-COMPETE AGREEMENTS

  - 8 Munich engineers have non-compete clauses with EUR 50K+ annual
    Karenzentschaedigung (post-contractual non-compete compensation)
  - Total annual non-compete compensation obligation: EUR 520,000
  - These obligations transfer to the acquirer under Section 613a BGB

7. PENSION / RETIREMENT OBLIGATIONS

NovaTech GmbH participates in a direct insurance (Direktversicherung) scheme.
No defined benefit pension obligations exist. Annual employer contributions
total EUR 384,000 (EUR 6,000 per eligible employee x 64 employees).

8. RECOMMENDATIONS

  (a) Budget EUR 4.1M for severance (worst case), not EUR 1.8M as reported
  (b) Prepare Section 613a notification letters in advance of signing
  (c) Engage labor counsel for potential works council formation support
  (d) Review all change-of-control employment clauses before SPA finalization
