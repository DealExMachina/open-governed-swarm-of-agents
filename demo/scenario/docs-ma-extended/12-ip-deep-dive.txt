1. SCOPE

Falk & Weber IP was retained by Meridian Capital Partners to conduct an
independent review of NovaTech AG's patent portfolio, with particular focus on
chain of title, encumbrances, and freedom-to-operate validation.

2. PORTFOLIO OVERVIEW — CONFIRMED

We confirm that NovaTech AG is the registered proprietor of seven (7) granted
European patents and two (2) pending PCT applications, as described in the
management-provided IP summary (Document 03).

3. CRITICAL FINDING: CO-OWNERSHIP DISPUTES

Our title search revealed material issues with two (2) of the seven granted
patents:

  (a) EP3512644 — "Federated Learning for Cross-Border Regulatory Analysis"
      Named inventors: Dr. Klaus Haber, Dr. Elena Voss
      ISSUE: Dr. Haber's invention assignment agreement, dated 2019-04-15,
      contains a carve-out for "inventions conceived outside the scope of
      employment duties." Dr. Haber's counsel (Rechtsanwalt Peter Lindner)
      has formally asserted that EP3512644 was conceived during Dr. Haber's
      prior academic research at ETH Zurich and that NovaTech's assignment
      is invalid. A formal opposition has not yet been filed but is threatened.

  (b) EP3847291 — "Predictive Risk Scoring for Regulatory Submissions"
      Named inventor: Dr. Klaus Haber (sole inventor)
      ISSUE: This patent is encumbered by a competing ownership claim. Dr.
      Haber's separation agreement (dated March 2024) includes an IP
      assignment clause, but our review of the agreement reveals that the
      assignment was conditioned on payment of a "fair compensation" amount
      of CHF 150,000 which NovaTech has not yet paid. Under Swiss employment
      law (Art. 332 OR), the assignment may therefore be voidable.

      Additionally, Axion Corp (Munich) has contacted Dr. Haber regarding
      licensing of EP3847291, suggesting potential third-party interest in
      challenging NovaTech's ownership.

4. FREEDOM-TO-OPERATE UPDATE

The original FTO opinion from Bauer & Richter (dated 2024-12-01) did not
consider Axion Corp's recently published patent application EP4102883
("AI-Driven Regulatory Risk Assessment Framework"), filed 2024-09-30. Our
preliminary review indicates potential overlap between Axion's claims and
NovaTech's Predictive Risk Scoring module. A full FTO update is recommended.

5. SEPARATION AGREEMENT DEFICIENCIES

We reviewed Dr. Klaus Haber's separation agreement and identified the following
deficiencies:

  - IP assignment consideration (CHF 150,000) remains unpaid after 12 months
  - Non-compete clause is limited to 6 months (expired September 2024)
  - No explicit waiver of moral rights under Swiss copyright law
  - Agreement does not reference EP3512644 by patent number

6. REMAINING PORTFOLIO

The remaining five (5) granted patents (EP3215847, EP3389102, EP3627891,
EP3741023, EP3952417) have clean chain of title with no identified
encumbrances. The two pending PCT applications are also unencumbered.

7. RECOMMENDATIONS

  (a) Immediately resolve the CHF 150,000 payment to Dr. Haber to cure the
      EP3847291 assignment deficiency
  (b) Engage Dr. Haber's counsel to negotiate a comprehensive IP settlement
      covering both disputed patents
  (c) Commission updated FTO analysis covering Axion Corp's EP4102883
  (d) Consider escrow or indemnification provisions in the SPA to cover
      IP-related contingencies (estimated exposure: EUR 5-15M)
