RESOLUTION OF 6 TRANSFERRED-PATIENT DISCREPANCIES (from Document 11):

  CASE 1: Patient 01-022 (PR vs SD)
    BICR determination: PARTIAL RESPONSE
    Rationale: Baseline measurement verified at 68mm (Site 01 correct).
    Site 07 re-measurement of 62mm inconsistent with protocol requirement
    to maintain original baseline. Sum at Cycle 6: 44mm (-35.3% from 68mm).
    RESOLVED: PR confirmed. Site 07 measurement overridden.

  CASE 2: Patient 01-034 (SD vs PD — new lesion dispute)
    BICR determination: PROGRESSIVE DISEASE
    Rationale: Retrospective review of Site 01 Cycle 4 scan by two
    independent radiologists: 8mm adrenal abnormality present but below
    RECIST measurability threshold (10mm). By Cycle 8 scan, lesion measured
    14mm and meets new lesion criteria per RECIST v1.1 Section 4.4.5.
    RESOLVED: PD confirmed. New lesion identified at Cycle 8.
    NOTE: PFS event date adjusted from Cycle 8 to Cycle 6 (when lesion
    first became unequivocally new, per retrospective BICR assessment).

  CASE 3: Patient 07-008 (PR vs PR — measurement discordance)
    BICR determination: PARTIAL RESPONSE
    BICR measurement: Target lesion sum 40mm (-37.5% from baseline 64mm)
    Rationale: Both sites correctly identified PR. Measurement difference
    (38mm vs 44mm) within known inter-reader variability. BICR measurement
    used as definitive value.
    RESOLVED: PR confirmed. No change in response category.

  CASE 4: Patient 01-041 (target lesion selection discrepancy)
    BICR determination: PARTIAL RESPONSE (using original 2-lesion set)
    Rationale: Per RECIST v1.1 Section 4.4.4, target lesions must be
    consistent from baseline. Site 01 original 2-lesion selection is
    definitive. Addition of third lesion by Site 07 is a protocol violation.
    RESOLVED: PR confirmed using Site 01 target lesion selection.
    CAPA: Site 07 radiologist retrained on RECIST transfer-of-care SOP.

  CASE 5: Patient 07-031 (SD vs PD — non-target progression)
    BICR determination: STABLE DISEASE
    Rationale: Non-target lesion assessment reviewed by 3 independent
    radiologists. Consensus (2 of 3): progression NOT unequivocal per
    RECIST v1.1. Pleural effusion increase noted but within physiologic
    variation. Site 01 PD call was premature.
    RESOLVED: SD confirmed. PFS event NOT recorded at this timepoint.
    Patient subsequently confirmed PD at Cycle 10 (new hepatic lesion).

  CASE 6: Patient 01-039 (NE vs SD — scan quality dispute)
    BICR determination: STABLE DISEASE
    Rationale: Scan quality assessed as adequate by 2 of 3 central
    reviewers. Motion artifact present but does not preclude measurement
    of target lesions. Site 01 NE call overly conservative.
    RESOLVED: SD confirmed. Patient evaluable for response.
