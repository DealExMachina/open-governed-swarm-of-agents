PRIMARY PHASE II ENDPOINT — OBJECTIVE RESPONSE RATE (ORR):
  Simon two-stage design threshold: >= 54 responses in 180 patients

  ORX-4471 arm (mITT, n=116):
    Complete Response (CR):         4 (3.4%)
    Partial Response (PR):         35 (30.2%)
    Stable Disease (SD):           42 (36.2%)
    Progressive Disease (PD):      30 (25.9%)
    Not Evaluable (NE):            5 (4.3%)

    ORR (CR+PR):                   39/116 = 33.6%
    95% CI:                        25.2% - 42.8%
    Disease Control Rate:          81/116 = 69.8%

  Docetaxel arm (mITT, n=58):
    CR: 0, PR: 8, SD: 22, PD: 24, NE: 4
    ORR: 8/58 = 13.8% (95% CI: 6.1-25.4%)

  ORR difference: 19.8% (95% CI: 8.4-31.2%, p<0.001)

  ** Phase II ORR threshold MET: 39 + 8 = 47 responses in ITT **
  NOTE: Using ORX-4471 arm alone, 39 responses exceeds Stage 2 threshold of
  27 responses (adjusted for 116 evaluable per arm).

SECONDARY ENDPOINT — PROGRESSION-FREE SURVIVAL (PFS):
  ORX-4471 arm:
    Median PFS:     6.8 months (95% CI: 5.4-8.2)
    6-month PFS:    54.2% (95% CI: 44.8-62.8)
  Docetaxel arm:
    Median PFS:     4.1 months (95% CI: 3.2-5.4)
    6-month PFS:    31.0% (95% CI: 19.6-43.0)

  Hazard Ratio:    0.62 (95% CI: 0.45-0.85)
  Log-rank p:      0.003

  ** PFS EXCEEDS historical control of 4.2 months **
  Comparison to prior studies:
    Docetaxel historical (TAX 317):     2.7 months
    Nivolumab (CheckMate-017):          3.5 months
    Atezolizumab (OAK):                4.0 months
    ORX-4471 (this study):             6.8 months

OVERALL SURVIVAL (immature):
  ORX-4471 arm:     Median not reached, 6-month OS 78.4%
  Docetaxel arm:    Median not reached, 6-month OS 68.9%
  HR: 0.71 (95% CI: 0.44-1.14, p=0.16) — not yet significant

SUBGROUP ANALYSES (PFS, ORX-4471 arm):
  PD-L1 >= 50%:    HR 0.48 (95% CI: 0.31-0.74) — strong benefit
  PD-L1 1-49%:     HR 0.72 (95% CI: 0.44-1.18) — trend toward benefit
  PD-L1 < 1%:      HR 1.04 (95% CI: 0.48-2.26) — no apparent benefit
  TMB >= 10:        HR 0.51 (95% CI: 0.32-0.81) — strong benefit
  KRAS mutant:      HR 0.58 (95% CI: 0.34-0.99) — significant benefit
  STK11 mutant:     HR 0.89 (95% CI: 0.48-1.64) — attenuated benefit

BIOSTATISTICIAN ASSESSMENT:
  1. Phase II efficacy criteria met. ORR and PFS significantly exceed control.
  2. Benefit appears concentrated in PD-L1-high and TMB-high subgroups.
  3. Recommend proceeding to Phase III expansion with PD-L1 stratification.
  4. Safety analysis pending concurrent DSMB review (see Document 09).

  NOTE: This analysis uses safety data AS REPORTED at data cutoff. Any
  subsequent corrections to individual patient data (see Document 08) will
  require a sensitivity analysis to assess impact on efficacy conclusions.
