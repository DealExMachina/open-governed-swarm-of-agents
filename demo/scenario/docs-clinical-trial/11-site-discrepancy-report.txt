BACKGROUND:
  During routine blinded independent central review (BICR) of tumor response
  assessments, discrepancies were identified between site-level and central
  assessments for patients who transferred care between participating sites.
  This report documents contradictory RECIST v1.1 assessments for 6 patients
  with cross-site transfers involving Site 01 (Memorial Sloan Kettering) and
  Site 07 (Cedars-Sinai).

DISCREPANT CASES:

  CASE 1: Patient 01-022 (transferred to Site 07 at Month 6)
    Site 01 assessment (Cycle 4):  Partial Response (PR)
      Target lesion sum: 42mm (baseline 68mm, -38.2%)
    Site 07 assessment (Cycle 6):  Stable Disease (SD)
      Target lesion sum: 48mm (baseline recalculated as 62mm, -22.6%)
    DISCREPANCY: Different baseline measurements used. Site 01 measured right
    upper lobe lesion at 32mm; Site 07 re-measured at 26mm on same scan.

  CASE 2: Patient 01-034 (transferred to Site 07 at Month 8)
    Site 01 assessment (Cycle 6):  Stable Disease (SD)
      No new lesions identified
    Site 07 assessment (Cycle 8):  Progressive Disease (PD)
      New lesion identified: left adrenal, 12mm
    DISCREPANCY: Site 07 identified adrenal lesion as NEW. Review of Site 01
    Cycle 4 scan shows 8mm adrenal abnormality not reported as lesion.

  CASE 3: Patient 07-008 (transferred to Site 01 at Month 5)
    Site 07 assessment (Cycle 3):  Partial Response (PR)
      Target lesion sum: 38mm (baseline 64mm, -40.6%)
    Site 01 assessment (Cycle 5):  Partial Response (PR)
      Target lesion sum: 44mm (baseline 64mm, -31.3%)
    DISCREPANCY: Both report PR but measurements differ by 6mm on comparable
    scans. Within 5mm measurement variability threshold? Borderline.

  CASE 4: Patient 01-041 (transferred to Site 07 at Month 7)
    Site 01 assessment (Cycle 5):  Partial Response (PR)
      Two target lesions measured per RECIST
    Site 07 assessment (Cycle 7):  Partial Response (PR)
      Three target lesions measured per RECIST
    DISCREPANCY: Site 07 added a third target lesion (mediastinal lymph node)
    not tracked by Site 01. Different target lesion selection violates RECIST
    v1.1 Section 4.4.4 (consistency of target lesion assessment).

  CASE 5: Patient 07-031 (transferred to Site 01 at Month 9)
    Site 07 assessment (Cycle 6):  Stable Disease (SD)
      Non-target lesions: present, not unequivocal progression
    Site 01 assessment (Cycle 8):  Progressive Disease (PD)
      Non-target lesions: unequivocal progression per investigator
    DISCREPANCY: Contradictory assessment of non-target lesion progression.
    RECIST v1.1 Section 4.4.6 "unequivocal" criterion subjectively applied.

  CASE 6: Patient 01-039 (transferred to Site 07 at Month 6)
    Site 01 assessment (Cycle 4):  Not Evaluable (NE)
      Scan quality insufficient per Site 01 radiologist
    Site 07 assessment (Cycle 4):  Stable Disease (SD)
      Same scan re-read by Site 07 radiologist as evaluable
    DISCREPANCY: Same imaging study assessed as evaluable by one site and
    not evaluable by another.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS:
  1. RECIST v1.1 measurement variability: Known inter-reader variability of
     10-15% for target lesion measurements (Schwartz et al., Eur J Cancer 2009).
  2. Target lesion selection: Protocol Section 8.3 requires consistent target
     lesion tracking, but transfers create ambiguity.
  3. Non-target assessment subjectivity: "Unequivocal progression" criterion
     inherently subjective per RECIST v1.1 guidelines.
  4. No standardized transfer-of-care imaging protocol in the current protocol.

IMPACT ASSESSMENT:
  Of 6 discrepant cases:
    - 2 cases affect response classification (Cases 1, 2)
    - 1 case affects PFS event determination (Case 5)
    - 3 cases are measurement discrepancies within expected variability

  Total discrepant assessments across study: 17 of 348 assessments (4.9%)
  Discrepancies involving transferred patients: 6 of 17 (35.3%)

RECOMMENDATIONS:
  1. Central radiology review (BICR) adjudication for all 6 cases.
  2. Protocol amendment to include transfer-of-care imaging SOP.
  3. All transferred patients to have BICR as primary assessment going forward.
  4. Cross-site imaging harmonization workshop (see Document 16).
