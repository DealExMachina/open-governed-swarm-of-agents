REASON FOR THIS AMENDED CONSENT:
  New safety information has become available since you signed the original
  consent form. This updated consent form describes changes to the risk
  information and monitoring procedures for ORX-4471.

SECTION 5: RISKS AND SIDE EFFECTS (REVISED)

  5.3 Liver-Related Side Effects (Hepatotoxicity)

  PREVIOUS LANGUAGE (ICF Version 3.0):
    "Liver enzyme elevations (hepatotoxicity) are UNCOMMON with ORX-4471.
    In the Phase I study, 1 of 18 patients (5.6%) experienced a serious liver
    enzyme elevation that required a pause in treatment. Your liver function
    will be monitored with blood tests during the study."

  REVISED LANGUAGE (ICF Version 4.0):
    "Liver enzyme elevations (hepatotoxicity) are a COMMON side effect of
    ORX-4471. In the Phase II study, approximately 8 out of 100 patients
    (7.8%) experienced serious liver enzyme elevations (Grade 3 or higher)
    that required a pause or change in treatment. One patient discontinued
    ORX-4471 due to liver problems.

    Serious liver enzyme elevations can occur at any time during treatment
    but most commonly occur within the first 4 months (Cycles 1-6).

    To monitor your liver function, you will have blood tests:
      - Before each treatment cycle (every 3 weeks)
      - On Day 8 (one week after treatment) during your first 6 cycles
      - Additional blood tests if your liver enzymes become elevated
      - You may be referred to a liver specialist (hepatologist) if your
        liver enzymes rise significantly

    If your liver enzymes become elevated:
      - Your treatment may be paused until your levels return to normal
      - Your dose may be reduced from 200mg to 150mg
      - If the problem recurs, your treatment with ORX-4471 will be stopped
      - You may be given steroids to help your liver recover

    In rare cases, severe liver injury can be life-threatening. You should
    contact your study doctor immediately if you experience: yellowing of
    your skin or eyes, dark urine, severe fatigue, nausea, or abdominal pain."

  5.4 Lung-Related Side Effects (Pneumonitis) — UNCHANGED from Version 3.0

  5.5 Other Immune-Related Side Effects — UNCHANGED from Version 3.0

SECTION 7: MONITORING PROCEDURES (REVISED)

  7.1 Blood Tests

  PREVIOUS SCHEDULE:
    - Before treatment: Every 3 weeks
    - Liver function: Every 3rd cycle (every 9 weeks)

  REVISED SCHEDULE:
    - Before treatment: Every 3 weeks
    - Liver function: Every cycle (every 3 weeks) PLUS Day 8 of Cycles 1-6
    - Additional tests if liver enzymes are elevated

  This means you will have approximately 6 additional blood draws during
  the first 18 weeks of treatment compared to the original schedule.

SECTION 9: NEW INFORMATION DISCLOSURE

  We will tell you about any new information that may affect your willingness
  to continue in this study. The safety information in this consent form was
  updated because:
    - Additional patients have been treated since you first enrolled
    - A review of safety data identified that liver enzyme elevations are
      more common than initially reported
    - The Data Safety Monitoring Board recommended enhanced liver monitoring

  The study doctors and the Data Safety Monitoring Board continue to believe
  that the potential benefits of ORX-4471 outweigh the risks for patients
  with your type of cancer, provided that enhanced monitoring is in place.
