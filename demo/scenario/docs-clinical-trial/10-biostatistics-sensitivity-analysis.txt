  To assess whether the data correction described in DCN-07-2024-001 (Document
  08) affects the primary efficacy conclusions reported in Document 07, and to
  quantify the impact on the integrated safety-efficacy profile.

ANALYSIS 1: EFFICACY SENSITIVITY (PRIMARY ENDPOINT — PFS)

  The corrected ALT values affect safety grading only. No patients were removed
  from the efficacy analysis, and tumor assessments for the three affected
  patients (07-012, 07-019, 07-024) are unchanged.

  PFS Analysis — Pre-correction data:
    ORX-4471 median PFS:  6.8 months (95% CI: 5.4-8.2)
    Docetaxel median PFS: 4.1 months (95% CI: 3.2-5.4)
    HR: 0.62 (95% CI: 0.45-0.85), p = 0.003

  PFS Analysis — Post-correction data (identical, no PFS events reclassified):
    ORX-4471 median PFS:  6.8 months (95% CI: 5.4-8.2)
    Docetaxel median PFS: 4.1 months (95% CI: 3.2-5.4)
    HR: 0.62 (95% CI: 0.45-0.85), p = 0.003

  CONCLUSION: Primary efficacy results UNCHANGED by data correction.

ANALYSIS 2: ORR SENSITIVITY

  graded) and switched to docetaxel. Tumor response at discontinuation was SD.

  ORR — Post-correction: 39/116 = 33.6% (95% CI: 25.2-42.8%)

ANALYSIS 4: BENEFIT-RISK ASSESSMENT (Quantitative Framework)

  Using the BRAT (Benefit-Risk Action Team) framework:

  Benefit metric (PFS HR):         0.62 [unchanged]
  Risk metric (Gr3+ hepatotox):    7.8% [increased from 5.2%]

  Net clinical benefit score:
    Pre-correction:   NNT = 4.2, NNH = 19.2, NNT/NNH ratio = 0.22
    Post-correction:  NNT = 4.2, NNH = 12.8, NNT/NNH ratio = 0.33

  Interpretation: Benefit-risk ratio modestly less favorable but still
  strongly positive. For every patient harmed by Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity,
  approximately 3 patients benefit from progression-free survival advantage.

ANALYSIS 5: COMPARISON WITH HISTORICAL BENCHMARKS

  Hepatotoxicity rates for approved checkpoint inhibitors in NSCLC:
    Nivolumab (CheckMate-017):     3.2% Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity
    Pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-010):   3.8% Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity
    Atezolizumab (OAK):           1.6% Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity
    ORX-4471 (pre-correction):     5.2% Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity
    ORX-4471 (post-correction):    7.8% Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity

  ORX-4471 post-correction rate is higher than approved monotherapies, consistent
  with dual checkpoint mechanism (anti-PD-L1 + anti-CTLA-4). Comparable to
  ipilimumab/nivolumab combination (CheckMate-227: 9.2%).
