STUDY OVERVIEW:
  Phase I, open-label, 3+3 dose-escalation study of ORX-4471 in patients with
  advanced solid tumors refractory to standard therapy. Conducted at Memorial
  Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (single site).

DOSE LEVELS EVALUATED:
  Level 1:  50mg IV q3w   (n=3, 0 DLTs)
  Level 2: 100mg IV q3w   (n=3, 0 DLTs)
  Level 3: 150mg IV q3w   (n=3, 0 DLTs)
  Level 4: 200mg IV q3w   (n=6, 2 DLTs)  <-- MTD
  Level 5: 300mg IV q3w   (n=3, 3 DLTs)  <-- exceeded MTD

DOSE-LIMITING TOXICITIES at 200mg (n=6):
  Patient P1-016: Grade 3 pneumonitis (onset Day 22, resolved Day 41 with
    methylprednisolone 1mg/kg). Per CTCAE v5.0 criteria.
  Patient P1-018: Grade 3 hepatitis (ALT 287 U/L, 7.2x ULN; onset Day 15,
    resolved Day 38 with dose hold and supportive care).

  DLT rate at 200mg: 2/6 (33%) — within protocol-specified MTD definition
  (< 33% with >= 6 evaluable patients, boundary inclusive).

PHARMACOKINETICS AT 200mg (n=6):
  Cmax:     142.3 mcg/mL (CV 28%)
  AUC0-21d: 18,420 mcg*h/mL (CV 31%)
  t1/2:     14.2 days (range 11.8-17.1)
  Trough:   22.4 mcg/mL (above target receptor occupancy of 15 mcg/mL)

OVERALL SAFETY PROFILE (N=18, all dose levels):

RECOMMENDED PHASE II DOSE (RP2D):
  ORX-4471 200mg IV every 3 weeks

CONCLUSIONS:
  1. MTD established at 200mg q3w with manageable toxicity profile.
  2. DLTs were immune-mediated and resolved with standard management.
  3. Hepatotoxicity signal present but within expected range for checkpoint
     inhibitors (cf. KEYNOTE-001, CheckMate-017 historical rates 5-10%).
  4. PK supports q3w dosing with adequate trough concentrations.
