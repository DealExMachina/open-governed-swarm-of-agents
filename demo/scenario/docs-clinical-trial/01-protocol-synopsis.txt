TITLE: A Randomized, Open-Label, Phase II/III Study of ORX-4471 (Anti-PD-L1/CTLA-4
Bispecific Checkpoint Inhibitor) versus Standard-of-Care Docetaxel in Patients with
Previously Treated Locally Advanced or Metastatic Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

STUDY DESIGN:
  - Phase:              II/III (seamless adaptive design per FDA Guidance 2019)
  - Randomization:      2:1 (ORX-4471 : Docetaxel)
  - Blinding:           Open-label with blinded independent central review (BICR)
  - Planned enrollment: 450 patients across 12 investigative sites
  - Duration:           36 months (enrollment 12 months, follow-up 24 months)

PRIMARY ENDPOINT (Phase III):
  Progression-Free Survival (PFS) per RECIST v1.1 by BICR

SECONDARY ENDPOINTS:
  - Overall Response Rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1
  - Overall Survival (OS)
  - Duration of Response (DOR)
  - Disease Control Rate (DCR)
  - Safety and tolerability per CTCAE v5.0

STUDY DRUG:
  ORX-4471 200mg IV q3w (dose established in Phase I, IND-178432-P1)
  Maximum Treatment Duration (MTD): 200mg q3w as established in Protocol P1-ORX-4471

STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS:
  Phase II:  Simon two-stage design, H0: ORR <= 20%, H1: ORR >= 35%
             Stage 1: n=80, >= 20 responses to proceed
             Stage 2: n=180 total, >= 54 responses to declare success
  Phase III: Log-rank test, HR = 0.65, alpha = 0.025 (one-sided)
             Power: 90%, estimated events: 302
             One interim analysis at 60% information fraction (Lan-DeMets alpha spending)

DATA SAFETY MONITORING BOARD (DSMB):
  Independent DSMB with 5 members, charter per ICH E9(R1).
  Planned reviews: after 80, 180, and 300 patients evaluable for safety.

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE:
  This study is conducted in accordance with ICH-GCP E6(R2), 21 CFR Parts 11,
  50, 56, and 312, and applicable local regulations. All electronic records

INVARIANT: Phase I dose-finding data (Protocol P1-ORX-4471) constitutes the

modify Phase I measurements or conclusions. Any Phase III hypothesis revision
