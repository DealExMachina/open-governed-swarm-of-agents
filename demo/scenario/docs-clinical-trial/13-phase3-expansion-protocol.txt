SUMMARY OF CHANGES FROM VERSION 3.1:

This amendment incorporates: (A) enhanced hepatic monitoring per DSMB
recommendation DSMB-002, (B) dose modification criteria for hepatotoxicity,
(C) PD-L1 stratification for Phase III randomization, (D) transfer-of-care
imaging SOP, and (E) FDA Information Request responses.

CHANGE 1: ENHANCED HEPATIC MONITORING SCHEDULE (Section 6.4, NEW)

  Pre-amendment monitoring:
    ALT/AST: Cycles 1, 3, 5, then every 3 cycles (q9w)

  Post-amendment monitoring:
    ALT/AST: Every cycle (q3w) PLUS Day 8 of Cycles 1 through 6
    Total Bilirubin: Every cycle (q3w)
    ALP/GGT: Cycles 1-6, then every 3 cycles
    INR/Albumin: If ALT >3x ULN

  Applies to: All active patients and all newly enrolled patients.
  Effective immediately upon IRB approval at each site.

CHANGE 2: DOSE MODIFICATION FOR HEPATOTOXICITY (Section 6.5, REVISED)

  Grade 2 hepatotoxicity (ALT 3-5x ULN):
    - Hold treatment, monitor ALT weekly
    - Resume at same dose when ALT < 2x ULN
    - If recurs: resume at 150mg (dose level -1)

  Grade 3 hepatotoxicity (ALT 5-20x ULN):
    - Hold treatment, hepatology consultation mandatory within 48 hours
    - Monitor ALT twice weekly
    - Consider corticosteroids if immune-mediated hepatitis suspected
    - Resume at 150mg (dose level -1) when ALT < 2x ULN
    - If recurs at 150mg: permanent discontinuation

  Grade 4 hepatotoxicity (ALT >20x ULN):
    - Permanent discontinuation of ORX-4471
    - Initiate methylprednisolone 2mg/kg/day
    - Hepatology consultation within 24 hours
    - Report as SAE within 24 hours

  NEW — Hy's Law monitoring:
    If ALT >3x ULN AND total bilirubin >2x ULN (in absence of bile duct
    obstruction or other cause): permanent discontinuation, FDA notification
    within 15 calendar days per 21 CFR 312.32.

CHANGE 3: PD-L1 STRATIFICATION (Section 5.2, REVISED)

  Phase III randomization now stratified by:
    - PD-L1 TPS: >= 50% vs. 1-49% vs. < 1%
    - ECOG PS: 0 vs. 1
    - Prior lines of therapy: 1 vs. >= 2
    - Region: North America vs. Ex-North America (for future global expansion)

  Rationale: Phase II subgroup analysis (Document 07) demonstrated differential
  benefit by PD-L1 status. Stratification ensures balance and enables
  pre-specified subgroup analyses for regulatory submission.

CHANGE 4: TRANSFER-OF-CARE IMAGING SOP (Section 8.3.5, NEW)

  For patients transferring between sites:
    a) Receiving site must use ORIGINAL baseline measurements from enrolling site
    b) Target lesion selection must be maintained as per enrolling site
    c) Central radiology review (BICR) becomes PRIMARY assessment for all
       transferred patients
    d) Receiving site performs local read as secondary assessment only
    e) Any discrepancy between site and BICR reported within 5 business days

CHANGE 5: UPDATED STOPPING BOUNDARIES (Section 9.3, REVISED)

  Hepatotoxicity stopping boundary:
    Phase II (n=180):   Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity > 8% (unchanged)
    Phase III (n=450):  Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity > 10% (revised from 8%)

  Rationale: The 8% boundary was established based on monotherapy checkpoint
  inhibitor historical data. ORX-4471 dual-mechanism profile warrants comparison
  to combination checkpoint therapy (ipilimumab + nivolumab: ~9% Grade 3+
  hepatotoxicity). The revised boundary of 10% for Phase III reflects this
  updated risk-benefit context while maintaining patient safety.

  NOTE: This revision does NOT retroactively change the Phase II stopping
  boundary assessment. The Phase II exceedance (11.1% at 80-patient landmark)
  is documented as occurred. The revised boundary applies only to Phase III.
