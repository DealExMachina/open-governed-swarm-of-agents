      Protocol ONCO-RESOLVE-301 (IND-178432)

Dear Dr. Holloway,

The Division has reviewed IND Safety Report ISR-178432-2024-008 (filed
2024-11-15) regarding the data correction at Site 07 (Cedars-Sinai Medical
Center) and the resulting impact on hepatotoxicity safety assessments. The
Division requests the following information pursuant to 21 CFR 312.23(a)(11)
and 312.56(b). Please respond within 30 calendar days of receipt.

REQUEST 1: COMPLETE AUDIT TRAIL FOR CORRECTED ALT VALUES

  a) Provide screen captures of the EDC audit trail (Medidata Rave) showing:
     - Original data entry timestamps and user credentials
     - Correction timestamps and user credentials
     - Reason for change fields as entered
     - System-generated vs. manual audit trail entries
  b) Provide copies of source documents (hospital laboratory printouts)
     for patients 07-012, 07-019, and 07-024 at the relevant visit (C4D1).
  c) Confirm whether the EDC system meets 21 CFR Part 11 requirements for:
     - Unique user identification (Section 11.100)
     - Electronic signature attribution (Section 11.200)
     - Audit trail integrity (Section 11.10(e))

REQUEST 2: DATA INTEGRITY PROCEDURES

  a) Describe the data entry and verification procedures in effect at Site 07
     at the time of the original entry (August 2024), including:
     - Single vs. dual data entry practices
     - Source document verification (SDV) frequency and scope
     - Query management timelines
  b) Describe any changes to data integrity procedures implemented since
     discovery of the error (October 2024).
  c) Provide results of the cross-site audit of ALT/AST values referenced
     in DSMB recommendation #5 (DSMB Meeting DSMB-002). Were additional
     transcription errors identified at any of the 12 sites?

REQUEST 3: HEPATIC MONITORING PROTOCOL AMENDMENT

  a) Provide the amended hepatic monitoring schedule referenced in DSMB
     recommendations. The Division expects this to include, at minimum:
     - Frequency of ALT/AST monitoring
     - Pre-specified dose modification criteria
     - Criteria for treatment discontinuation
     - Hepatology consultation triggers
  b) Provide the timeline for protocol amendment submission (Amendment v3.2).
  c) Clarify how the amended monitoring will be applied:
     - To newly enrolled patients only, or retroactively to all active patients
     - Whether re-consent is required for active patients

REQUEST 4: SAFETY ANALYSIS VERSIONING

  a) For each safety analysis or report submitted under this IND since
     first-patient-first-visit, identify:
     - Which version of the ALT data was used (pre-correction or post-correction)
     - Whether conclusions change with the corrected data
  b) Provide the sensitivity analysis referenced in DSMB Meeting DSMB-002.
  c) Confirm that the Phase I safety summary (Protocol P1-ORX-4471) has NOT
     been modified as a result of Phase II findings.

REQUEST 5: STOPPING BOUNDARY ASSESSMENT

  a) The protocol specifies a hepatotoxicity stopping boundary of 8%. The
     Division notes that the corrected rate (reported variously as 7.8% and
     8.4% depending on evaluable population) may exceed this boundary.
     Provide:
     - Clear definition of the evaluable population for the stopping boundary
     - Recalculated rate using the protocol-specified population
     - If the boundary is exceeded, justification for continued enrollment
  b) Provide the DSMB meeting minutes, including the dissenting opinion
     referenced in the DSMB communication.

REQUEST 6: RECIST DISCREPANCIES

  a) The Division is aware of cross-site discrepancies in tumor response
     assessments (referenced in sponsor communications). Provide:
     - Number and nature of discrepant assessments
     - Resolution process and timeline
     - Impact on primary endpoint (PFS) assessment

RESPONSE TIMELINE:
  The sponsor is requested to respond within 30 calendar days of receipt
  (response due by 2025-01-02). Failure to respond may result in a clinical
  hold per 21 CFR 312.42.
