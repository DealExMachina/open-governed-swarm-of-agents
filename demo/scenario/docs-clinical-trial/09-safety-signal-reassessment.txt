REASON FOR EMERGENCY MEETING:
  Per DSMB Charter Section 4.3 (Unscheduled Reviews), an emergency meeting is
  convened when a data correction materially affects a safety threshold. Data
  Correction Notice DCN-07-2024-001 (Document 08) reports that three patients
  at Site 07 had ALT values corrected from 180 U/L to 378-384 U/L, changing
  their hepatotoxicity grade from Grade 2 to Grade 3.

RECALCULATED SAFETY DATA:

  HEPATOTOXICITY — 80-Patient Landmark (original DSMB-001 population):
    Pre-correction (reported in DSMB-001):
      Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity:   3/54 (5.6%) in ORX-4471 arm
    Post-correction:
      Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity:   6/54 (11.1%) in ORX-4471 arm

    ** EXCEEDS protocol-specified stopping boundary of 8% **
    Delta: +5.5 percentage points (from 5.6% to 11.1%)

  HEPATOTOXICITY — Full Phase II Population (n=116 ORX-4471 mITT):
    Pre-correction:
      Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity:   6/116 (5.2%)
    Post-correction:
      Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity:   9/116 (7.8%)
      Exact 95% CI:              3.6% - 14.2%

    ** Rate approaches but does not definitively exceed 8% boundary **
    ** Upper bound of 95% CI (14.2%) exceeds boundary **

  NOTE ON RATE DISCREPANCY: The rate of 8.4% referenced in the DSMB's initial
  alert notification was calculated using an intermediate evaluable population
  of 107 patients (9/107 = 8.4%). The mITT population of 116 yields 7.8%.
  Both calculations are presented for transparency.

HEPATOTOXICITY — COMPARISON TO PHASE I BASELINE (Document 02):
  Phase I Grade 3+ hepatotoxicity:   1/18 (5.6%)  [LOCKED, immutable]
  Phase II pre-correction:           5.2%          [consistent with Phase I]
  Phase II post-correction:          7.8%          [higher than Phase I]

  The DSMB notes that Phase I data (Document 02) remains the foundational
  safety baseline and is NOT subject to revision. The increased Phase II
  rate represents NEW safety information, not a correction of Phase I data.

PATIENT DISPOSITION FOR CORRECTED CASES:
  Patient 07-012: Dose held at Cycle 4, resumed Cycle 6 at 150mg (dose
    reduction per protocol). Currently on treatment, disease stable.
  Patient 07-019: Dose held at Cycle 4, resumed Cycle 7 at 150mg. Currently
    on treatment, partial response maintained.
  Patient 07-024: Dose permanently discontinued at Cycle 4 due to concurrent
    AST elevation (AST 312 U/L). Switched to docetaxel. Progressive disease.

DSMB DELIBERATION:
  The Board reviewed the following considerations:
  1. Corrected hepatotoxicity rate (7.8%) approaches but may not definitively
     exceed the 8% stopping boundary depending on evaluable population used.
  2. The stopping boundary was designed for prospective monitoring, not
     retroactive recalculation after data corrections.
  3. All three affected patients were managed appropriately at the clinical
     level regardless of the transcription error.
  4. Efficacy signal remains strong (PFS HR 0.62, Document 07).
  5. No hepatic deaths or Grade 4 hepatotoxicity events.

DSMB RECOMMENDATIONS:
  1. ** DO NOT STOP ENROLLMENT ** (Vote: 4-1, Dr. Osei dissenting)
  2. Implement expanded hepatic monitoring protocol:
     - ALT/AST monitoring every cycle (q3w) plus Day 8 of Cycles 1-6
     - Pre-defined dose modification algorithm for ALT >3x ULN
     - Mandatory hepatology consultation for ALT >5x ULN
  3. Request sponsor file protocol amendment (v3.2) incorporating enhanced
     hepatic monitoring within 30 days.
  4. Request sensitivity analysis from biostatistics (Document 10) to confirm
     efficacy conclusions unchanged by correction.
  5. Full audit of all safety laboratory data across all 12 sites.

DISSENTING OPINION (Dr. William Osei):
  "The corrected rate exceeds the pre-specified stopping boundary at the
  80-patient landmark. Protocol fidelity requires enrollment pause pending
  full audit. I recommend temporary enrollment hold until cross-site audit
  is complete."
