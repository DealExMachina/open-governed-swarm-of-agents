DSMB DELIBERATION:

  The Board considered the following:

  1. SAFETY: One treatment-related death (pneumonitis) in the ORX-4471 arm.
     This is within the expected range for checkpoint inhibitors (0.5-1.0%).
     The docetaxel arm also had one treatment-related death (sepsis). Overall
     safety profile acceptable and consistent with class effects.

  2. HEPATOTOXICITY: Grade 3+ rate of 7.0% is below both the original Phase
     II boundary (8%) and the Phase III boundary (10%). Enhanced monitoring
     has reduced severity and improved early detection. No Grade 4 events.
     Dr. Fischer (hepatology consultant) confirms management adequate.

  3. DATA INTEGRITY: The Site 07 transcription error has been fully resolved.
     Cross-site audit revealed no additional significant errors. Enhanced
     procedures are working effectively.

  4. EFFICACY: The interim analysis crosses the pre-specified efficacy boundary.
     Both PFS and OS favor ORX-4471. The Board notes that per the alpha-spending
     plan, the trial CAN be stopped early for efficacy.

  5. PHASE I BASELINE INTEGRITY: Phase I safety data (Document 02) remains
     unchanged and immutable. All Phase II/III safety findings documented as
     new information. The DSMB confirms no retroactive modifications have
     been made to Phase I data.

  6. BENEFIT-RISK: The benefit-risk profile is favorable. PFS and OS benefits
     are clinically meaningful. Safety is manageable with enhanced monitoring.

DSMB RECOMMENDATIONS:

  1. ** TRIAL CONTINUES TO PRIMARY ENDPOINT ** (Vote: Unanimous, 5-0)
     Despite crossing the interim efficacy boundary, the Board recommends
     continuing to the primary OS analysis to strengthen the dataset for
     regulatory submission and long-term safety characterization.

  2. ** BENEFIT-RISK ASSESSMENT: FAVORABLE ** (Vote: Unanimous, 5-0)
     The hepatotoxicity signal, while real, is manageable with enhanced
     monitoring. The efficacy benefit clearly outweighs the safety risk.

  3. ** HEPATIC MONITORING: ADEQUATE ** (Vote: Unanimous, 5-0)
     Enhanced monitoring per Amendment v3.2 is sufficient. No further
     modifications recommended.

  4. ** DATA INTEGRITY: RESOLVED ** (Vote: Unanimous, 5-0)
     Enhanced data entry and verification procedures are effective.
     No further action required beyond ongoing monitoring.

  5. The Board notes Dr. Osei's previous dissent (DSMB-002) is now moot
     given the completed cross-site audit, resolution of data integrity
     concerns, and hepatotoxicity rate below all stopping boundaries.
     Dr. Osei concurs.
